# **Buying an antenna for better mobile Internet signal quality**

## Welcome

Hi, I'm Sanjay. Together with Gabriel, another gadget friend, we made our first purchase of a 3/4G antenna, after researching commercial solutions to the connectivity problem.

In this solution I am going to give you some recommendations on how to buy a 3/4G antenna. 

## **What is a 3G or 4G antenna?**

3G and 4G are abbreviations for the set of technologies required for voice and data transmission in mobile telephony. 

This set includes, from the types of cell phones and antennas, to communication protocols between them, which are necessary to send voice or data over cellular telephony. 

I will not go into the details, but, out of curiosity, it is worth knowing that *G* stands for *generation* and the numbers are the third and fourth generation of these technologies. 

Previous generations did not allow sending data, only voice, like radio. So, for example, old cell phones -which in Colombia we call "arrows"- are not used to receive Internet data. To be able to connect to the Internet, devices need to be cell phones with 3G technology, or higher, i.e. smart phones or *smartphones*. 

## What is a 3/4G antenna for?

I recently understood that the Internet signal, which reaches cell phones, travels through invisible electromagnetic waves. Cell phones have a small antenna inside, which captures these waves. This allows us to access the Internet. 

Gabriel and I have found that buying a commercial antenna, 3G or 4G, is one of the best solutions to have a stable and reliable Internet connection. Especially, in a fixed location such as a home, school, library or store, making use of mobile telephony in places where coverage is low. 

What the antenna does, quite simply, is to capture and amplify the cellular-specific data signal (3G or 4G), which comes in electromagnetic waves, through the air, from a higher place than where you are - over trees, buildings or hills - and bring it down, via a cable, to your height. 

Once at that point, the signal can be shared in that space by generating a WiFi network, or by cable, using a [*modem*](https://www.notion.so/1512bd68c5b680359894c66ee55ee27d?pvs=21) or *router*. I know these terms may seem confusing, but, as you go through these recommendations, you will have more clarity on them.

## **Before we start, a thought**

Sometimes, we decide to buy the cheapest because we prefer to save money on a purchase. As they say, "cheap is expensive" and in the case of technology, such as an antenna, this tends to be true. 

We have seen many antennas that become garbage, do not fulfill their function (such as those of satellite TV providers), are installed in cities and rural areas, generating technological waste that, besides being ugly, is never removed or reused. 

It is important that you have clear, also, which are the care and maintenance that your purchase can cause you. Sometimes we buy something and, when it needs to be maintained, we do not have the time or resources to deal with it and it may become unusable. When you agree with others, you can share the care and distribute the expenses, it will make them lighter and more bearable.

Make sure that the antenna you buy is suitable for the conditions of your context and that it is the best option for you.  

## **What do you need?**

- Recognize the geography of the place where you need signal.
- Identify the operator or operators that work best in the region.
- An antenna provider.
- Some money.
- Get together with your community to identify the best option.

## **How to do it?**

Here are the 4 moments of what we consider to say what type of 3G/4G antenna to buy. 

- **Moment 1: How do I know if I need a 3/4G antenna?**
    
    There are places where the cellular signal is very weak. You can confirm it on your cell phone, on the connection bars: you will see a single bar or something that says *H+,* *E or* even that there is no signal. 
    
    However, when you move, a few meters or a few kilometers, the signal appears or improves and you start to see bars with the 3G or 4G symbol on your cell phone. 
    
    If this is your case, there may be cell phone towers around you that you do not have direct visibility to, and therefore you are not able to connect to the 3G/4G network from the initial location. 
    
    Buying a 3G/4G antenna will allow you to have a stable Internet connection, but there must be cellular coverage in the area. 
    
    It is not a cheap solution, but it is the most reliable way to establish an Internet access point for your community, if you are in places with low cellular coverage. 
    
    **If there is no cellular coverage, you will not achieve connectivity with one of these antennas.** To get it, you will have to resort to solutions such as those described in the solution [How to buy a satellite internet service for non-interconnected places?](/como-comprar-una-antena-satelital-solutions-db) where there are several satellite solutions, although they tend to be more expensive. 
    
- **Moment 2: According to what you need, these are the questions to ask yourself**
    - **Where am I geographically located?**
        
        
        The first thing is to understand the geography of where you are located. What you are trying to identify are the cell phone towers that could provide you with signal. For 3/4G antennas to work, generally, they must be able to "see" a tower, or, at the very least, they should be pointed at the same location. 
        
        If you live in a region with a lot of mountains it will be a little more difficult to get a good signal, as opposed to a flat place. 
        
        If possible, look for the nearest cell tower: look for tall towers with antennas. They are usually located on the tops of the highest mountains to allow for better coverage.
        
        If you don't know where the nearest cell phone tower is, you can also ask the neighbors, maybe they have already identified it.

    - **Which cell phone providers have coverage in this area?**
        
        In rural places, or far from urban centers, the coverage of cell phone operators can be quite regular. 
        
        Be sure to find the one that works best in the region. Ask your neighbors which one works best for them. Identify which ones have better coverage, that is, which provide a more stable and faster service. 
        
        
💡 Keep in mind that the coverage of these providers changes over time, as they install new repeater antennas or as more people subscribe to their services, saturating their signal.

        This information is important for the antenna provider: make sure that the modem you receive has open bands, so that you can use the operator of your choice, without restrictions.
        
    - **Where are the nearest cell phone antenna towers located?**
        
        
        Use a cell phone application to identify which cell phone towers are closest to you. 
        
        To do this, search Google Play or the Apple Store for "Cell Antennas" applications, download one and use it to identify which antennas, and from which operators, are the closest to where you are located.
        
        If there is no signal in the place where you want to test, you can download the map of the antennas in a place with connection and carry it on your cell phone. It will allow you to interpret it by matching the geographical North and the North of the map.

    - **Which is the space I want to provide with internet?**
        
        To decide which is the place where you are going to leave the *modem* connected, emitting WiFi signal, it is important that you take into account the geographical location and its connection to electricity. 
        
        We recommend you to think about public spaces such as schools, libraries or community halls, since the internet in these spaces can benefit more people. 
        
        If you want the connection for your home, it may be worthwhile to put it in a space such as the living room or dining room, rather than in the bedrooms. Spaces in the house intended for relaxation should be free from the electromagnetic fields of the device, which can affect it, and, on the other hand, physical obstacles can affect the signal in the rest of the house.
        
    - **How much, and for what purpose, will those who connect use the Internet?**
        
        Defining how much they will use the Internet and what they want to use it for is important to know what to expect from the antenna they are buying.
        
        To refine your expectations, you can read [¿Cómo se mide el uso de Internet?](/como-se-mide-el-uso-de-internet-inspiration-db) 
        
    - **How much money can I or we count on to buy an antenna?**
        
        In Colombia, in the year 2024, one of the most powerful antennas in the market, with a robust cable of 15 meters long, plus the modem, cost around COP 1,300,000 or USD 300. 
        
        But it is not necessary to face this expense alone, think that the more people contribute to the purchase of the antenna, the cheaper it will be per person. So we encourage you to talk to your family and neighbors to reach an agreement and share the signal.
        
- **Momento 3: Qué necesito saber para responder estas preguntas**
    - **Reconoce la topografía del lugar**
        
        Después de responder dónde estás, es importante, si es posible, obtener la ubicación precisa del lugar en donde te quieres conectar.
        
        Con la ayuda de una aplicación de ‣, ubica las coordenadas exactas del lugar donde quieres hacer la instalación de la antena.
        
        Este dato te será útil al momento de la compra. Los proveedores suelen tener acceso a bases de datos actualizadas sobre la cobertura de los diferentes proveedores de telefonía, así como la ubicación, lo que les permite asesorarte, con mayor precisión, sobre el tipo de antena que mejor responde a a tus necesidades específicas.
        
        Tu teléfono puede tener una aplicación para ubicar tus coordenadas. Si usas Android, ve a Google Maps. Si tienes un iPhone, ve a Mapas. 
        
        Busca tu punto de ubicación y así podrás acceder a las coordenadas exactas del lugar donde estás. Por último, apunta este dato para tenerlo a mano cuando lo necesites.

    - **¿Dónde comprar la antena?**
        
        Puedes hacer un SOLE, para realizar una búsqueda en Internet, en el que tu Gran Pregunta contenga términos como *antena amplificadora de señal 4g*. La búsqueda debería llevarte a encontrar distribuidores cercanos. 
        
        Cuando hables con la empresa, que distribuye la tecnología, ten todos los datos que puedas, a mano. Juntos encontrarán la mejor solución para el lugar donde vas a montar tu punto de acceso a Internet.
        
    - **¿Qué te deben entregar cuando hayas hecho la compra de tu antena?**
        
        Tienes que tener en cuenta que, para que la antena funcione, es necesario que esté conectada a un aparato. Así que lo mínimo que debe contener tu kit de antena es:
        
        1. Una antena 3/4G
        2. Los elementos básicos de montaje
        3. El cable de conexión
        4. Un módem 3/4 G
        
        **Antena**
        
        La antena necesaria es una [***tipo yagi***](/antena-tipo-yagi-yagi-antenna-conceptorio). Puede ser similar a la de la imagen arriba, o puede, también, estar encapsulada en un protector plástico, que protegerá la antena y sus conexiones de las condiciones climáticas, alargando su vida útil.
        
        Una cosa que hay que tener en cuenta, con la antena, es su potencia. Este dato está expresado en Db: una antena de 65Db tendrá más potencia que una de 15Db. Como regla general, cuanto más lejos se encuentra la antena del proveedor, será necesaria una antena con mayor potencia para poder establecer una conexión.

        **Cable**
        
        El cable para conectar la antena al módem es parecido al cable coaxial, que se usa en las conexiones de televisión. Sin embargo, no es el mismo. Hay una amplia variedad de tipos de cable, pero, mínimamente, en este tipo de aplicaciones, se deben usar cables como el [RG-6](/cable-rg6-rg6-cable-conceptorio). Ahora bien, es recomendable usar cables blindados, que minimicen la pérdida de señal y reduzcan la producción de ruido en el sistema.
        
        No debes modificar el cable que te entregue el proveedor de ninguna manera: el empalme entre cable y conectores en los extremos se lleva a cabo de manera que se minimice el ruido en el sistema, **cualquier alteración puede hacer totalmente inservibles todos los componentes**.

        [**Módem / Router](/modem-router-o-enrutador-modem-or-router-conceptorio)**
        Normalmente, los kits de antenas, vienen con un [módem y un enrutador](/modem-router-o-enrutador-modem-or-router-conceptorio), que te permitirá conectarte y compartir la señal de la red móvil captada por la antena. 
        
        Si ya cuentas con un módem, al momento de comprar, verifica que este pueda conectarse a la antena y, en dado caso, que el proveedor te envíe los adaptadores necesarios para realizar la conexión.
        
    
- **Moment 4: And then... let's make the decision!**
    
    Depending on your geographical conditions, what you want to use it for and your budget, ask the vendor for all possible options. 
    
    Don't forget to talk about the care and maintenance of the devices, especially the costs involved and how often you will have to bear them. 
    
    The most expensive one is not necessarily the one that will serve you best. 
    

## Don't be fooled

Sometimes, salespeople will want to offer you more sophisticated and expensive solutions than you will need. 

Ask a lot of questions. It is the only way to learn and create a criterion to find the best possible option. 

## **How much is enough?**

Buying an antenna with the necessary characteristics to access the mobile network will allow you to have a stable connection with which, for example, to meet to learn how to learn, in a group, using the Internet. 

You can also watch movies, consult a doctor about a situation that may occur, communicate with family or friends... so find new ways to use the Internet!

Also, when you have a more stable connection, doing SOLE will be easier.

A single antenna and a single connection, in a group, will be more than enough to enjoy all the options that the internet has to offer. Moreover, using the internet on shared computers will be safer, more private and more fun.
