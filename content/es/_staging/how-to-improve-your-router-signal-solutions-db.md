# **Some tips to get the most out of your router**

## **Welcome**

Hi! I'm Manu, the SOLE Colombia accountant, and I've also been very frustrated about losing my Internet signal right in the middle of an important meeting or a SOLE.  

## What is improving your router signal?

Just when you need it the most, the connection disappears, has it happened to you? It has happened to me more than once. But don't worry, because you're about to learn how to improve your [**router**](/modem-router-o-enrutador-modem-or-router-conceptorio) 's signal so that it happens less often.

Plus, I'll show you how to optimize your [**Router**](/modem-router-o-enrutador-modem-or-router-conceptorio) settings so you can surf faster by changing the [**DNS**](/dns-sistema-de-nombres-de-dominio-dns-domain-name-system-conceptorio) servers and adjusting the [**WiFi**](/wifi-fidelidad-inalambrica-wifi-wireless-fidelity-conceptorio) channel. This is not difficult and you can do it from home! So get ready, we're going to get the most out of your network.

## **What is it for?**

I'm going to walk you through how to get the most out of your **router**. Together, we will improve the distribution of the **WiFi** signal in your space, achieving a more stable connection, so it won't drop out when you need it most, whether it's for a meeting, a class, or a SOLE session. No more interrupted signals when you're researching a Big Question!

Remember that these settings will not increase your contracted speed, but they will help you get a better signal to every corner of your home by optimizing the DNS.

## **What do you need?**

- Use an Internet service that connects through a **Router**.
- The Router's access keys.
- A computer connected to the network.
- A little patience (hopefully not too much!).
- **Cacharrera** attitude**.**

## How to do it?

Now I am going to take you step by step to improve your WiFi signal. Trust me, something will have to change!

    The first thing to do is to measure the speed of your current connection. This will allow you to compare the "before" and "after" adjustments. Download the [**Fast**](https://fast.com/es/) application or use the speed meter of your choice, and perform tests from various points in your home.

- **Moment 2: Explore your space and locate the Router**
    
    It may seem obvious, but we often forget the simplest thing: get close to the **Router**. If you are organizing a SOLE session or have several devices connected, make sure they are as close as possible to get the strongest signal. 
    
    Where you place the **router** is key. Be sure to place it in an elevated spot, such as on a table, or even on the ceiling. Ideally, it should be in a central location, not in a corner or near a window, where it is often left by installers. Moving the **router** to a more strategic place will allow the signal to be distributed more evenly throughout the house.

- **Moment 4: Configure the DNS**
    
    
    What's that? 
    
    **DNS is like an address book that helps your computer find websites faster. By using faster DNS servers, such as Google's or Cloudflare's, you ensure that pages load faster.** Your Internet provider's default servers are not always the most optimal, so switching to more efficient ones can improve your browsing experience.

    
    Now let's do something interesting: configure the **DNS**. This will help the web pages you visit load faster, since **DNS** are the directories of IP addresses that the **Router** queries when browsing. 
    
     If you want to change to faster and more reliable **DNS** servers you will make your Internet queries faster. To achieve this, follow these steps:
    
    1. Access the administration interface of your **Router**. To do this, in the network settings of your computer, look for the **gateway**. There you will find an IP address, something like **192.168.0.1** or **192.168.1.1.**
    2. Log in with the credentials, which are usually **admin/admin**, or check your device's manual for the default passwords.
    3. Change the values to **1.1.1.1.1**, **1.0.0.1** (from **Cloudflare**) and **8.8.8.8.8** (from **Google**).
    4. Open your browser and enter that address in the address bar. This will take you to the **Router** control panel.
    5. Find the **Network Settings** section and, there, the **DNS Servers** option.
    6. Save the changes and restart the **Router**.
    
    Great! Now your network will use these DNS servers, which are faster and more reliable.
    
- **Step 5: Configure the WiFi channel**
    
    Another key setting is the **WiFi** channel configuration. Channels determine the frequency on which the **Router** broadcasts the signal. To avoid interference with other nearby networks, set your **Router** to the recommended channels: **1**, **6** or **11:**
    
    1. Enter your **Router**'s configuration interface again.
    2. Look for the **Wireless** section.
    3. Choose one of the recommended channels: **1**, **6** or **11**.
    4. Save the changes and restart the **Router**.
    
    With this setting, you will reduce interference and improve signal quality. Okay, we're almost done!
