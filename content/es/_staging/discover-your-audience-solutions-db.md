# In communication, the audience rules

## Welcome

Hello! 😊 

I'm Manu, the storyteller of SOLE Colombia. In all the stories I tell, I always keep in mind to whom I am addressing what I want to say... have you ever wondered how to really reach the people who will value your message the most, or who will participate in your projects? 

In this solution we are going to discover your audience together, are you ready?

## What is discovering your audience?

Knowing your audience means understanding in depth who are the people you are targeting with your content. When you know your audience, you can focus your efforts where you know they will have an impact. What's the point of sending an email if you want to invite your family? 

Knowing your audience, you know by heart the shortest way to get straight to the heart of your people, and connect with the right people, so that your message reaches further. 

## **What is it good for?**

When you know who you are targeting and know them well, it allows you to tailor your message, tone and communication style to resonate with the people you are targeting and what you say to them will resonate with them. 

You will create content that is more relevant to those people, appropriate to their interests and needs, useful, interesting and valuable to them... you will certainly improve the way they perceive you, but, most importantly, you will be able to create genuine and authentic connections because your message will go straight to their hearts.

## What do you need?

- Paper
- Stylus/Pencil
- Enthusiasm
- Cell phone or computer (for writing and carrying out the activities)

## How to do it?

- **Moment 1: Define your 'target audience' in detail**
    
    Before you start, clearly establish who are the people you want to reach with your message or call... Think about the key characteristics of the people you want to reach. 
    
    Take out your pencil or pen and answer on a sheet of paper:
    
    - What are the interests, needs or challenges of your audience?
    - What kind of people would be most interested in what you have to offer?
    
    See how I did with this exercise:
    
    Ilustración_sin_título(12).mp4.mp4)
    
- **Moment 2: Create your personas, dig deep to create your ideal person.**
    
    Taking into account the answer to the questions below, create detailed profiles of your target audience. This will help you visualize and better understand who you are targeting and what motivates them.
    
    1. **Discover their characteristics:** write down the basic qualities of your ideal audience, such as age, gender, geographic location, education level, etc.
    2. **Interests and motivations:** describe what motivates or interests these people, what are they looking for or need?
    3. **Behaviors and preferences:** where do they usually spend their time, what type of content do they consume or what activities do they enjoy?
    4. Challenges: what are the things that prevent them from getting to where they want to go or where you want to invite them? what are the challenges they face?
    
    When you have completed this exercise it will be easier to visualize your ideal audience. See how I did it:
    
    Ilustración_sin_título(13).mp4.mp4)
    
- **Moment 3: Observe and listen to your people, look for answers.**
    
    Now that you have a clear idea of who your potential participants are, it's time to observe and listen.
    
    1. Start interacting with your potential audience through **surveys**, **interviews** or simply **observing their interactions** online. 
    2. Note any **responses, comments or patterns** that will help you better understand their needs and preferences and [disseminate your message,](https://www.notion.so/60ca12b031b242549df2b865edd9a684?pvs=21) taking into account their reactions or behaviors. 

## How do you know if this solution works?

It is very important that you come up with ways to analyze the way your audience lets you know that you are reaching them. How can you know? 

Observe: how do the people who interact with your messages behave? Talk to them directly, invent easy ways to collect data about their experiences, opinions, expectations... you can design a survey or form to get more data and treat it statistically. 

Looking for patterns, trends and motivations can lead you to more qualitative analysis, where you can find more complex and interesting explanations about your audience. 

It's exciting to find the best way to communicate with the people you want to reach! Ask yourself questions about them constantly, so you can communicate better and better.

Is it working for you, write to tell me about it! 

## Why might this solution work?

Many times, as you get better at defining your audience, you realize that there are subgroups that you want to approach and, necessarily, you have to do it in a different way. 

For example, in SOLE Colombia we have a very large Community, in which there are different 'characters' to whom we speak. We can't talk to the Abuelitas in the same way we talk to the Cacharreros, because it simply wouldn't work. The content we create, aims to accommodate all of them, but to address specific messages to each group, building community from the affection we give to each group. 

We think a lot about the ways of communicating and the audiences we discover, that's what we do. Continually experimenting. Adapting to the findings. And doing it all over again. Many times.

It's fun!
