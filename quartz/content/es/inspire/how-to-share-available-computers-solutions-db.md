Sole Colombia

## Welcome

Hello, I'm Sanjay. I've been doing SOLE for ten years and have brought SOLE Colombia to what it is today. For years, I've seen how many computers are available collecting dust in school closets, or with restricted use because the person assigned to their custody believes that strict criteria must be met.

## What is sharing available computers and managing their lending?

With this solution, I want to focus on the importance of using the Internet in groups. It's a very different experience: it's shared use of devices, which opens the door for us to walk together along the paths of the Internet. You'll have the opportunity to experience devices that we all use and care for, which are the gateway to living better together, using the Internet. 

## What is it for?

Sharing computers benefits everyone. We don't need as much money to buy or maintain them because we can share the costs among more people, and more people can benefit from their use.

When we share computers and use them together, they become a safe place to learn and do things together: we take care of the spaces we enter, avoid dangerous portals or inappropriate content to protect others, better understand the information we review together, find common interests, interact with each other instead of just with machines... we turn technology into a park where everyone can play. SOLE is a perfect tool for sharing your computers with an interesting and common purpose: solving Big Questions. Want to know how? Go to [https://www.solecolombia.org/solea](https://www.solecolombia.org/solea) and find out.

## What do you need?

- Computers to share.
- Place with electricity.
- Generosity.

## How to do it?

- **Step 1: Managing computers**
    
    First, you need computers to share, which you can find in a public library or school. Normally there are government programs that provide these computers, and they often remain stored and unused due to lack of knowledge.
    
    Once you identify the place, you must find the person in charge, who will be the key person for your success. Tell them about your purpose for using the computers, such as doing SOLE or any other purpose. Also, explain the difficulty or impossibility for your community to have access to their own computers. Then set a date and time when you will come with your community so that both parties are committed.

- **Step 2: Agree on rules for sharing**
    
    Once the place and computers are committed, gather your community and talk about the arrangements you've already made so they can see the prior work and share the responsibility with you. Then, discuss the minimum care they should take and ask if they can think of other types of care. Remember that this first time will be decisive for whether they will continue to lend you computers in the future.

- **Step 3: Let's share!**
    
    Contact the library manager or public space and go with your group to enjoy using the internet together. Always remember to enjoy the devices in a shared way, for example, 4 people can sit at the same computer.
    

## How do you know if this solution works?

This solution is a series of recommendations that give you a route to more easily manage computers in a public space to share with your community.

## Why might this solution work?

This solution will not allow you to get computers permanently, and in places with little government presence, you may not find places where they will lend you these computers.
