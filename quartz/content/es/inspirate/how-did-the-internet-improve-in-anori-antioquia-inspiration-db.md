On the banks of the river, between rocks and streams, the community of La Guayana, nestled in the mountains of Anorí, Antioquia, lives surrounded by majestic nature and a territory that fills them with pride. However, that same geography, which gives them wealth and beauty, also isolates them from the digital world.

[20240624_SOLE+WLH_Sintonización_Antioquia_LaGuayana (3).MP4](../../../¿Cómo mejoró el Internet en Anorí, Antioquia 1362bd68c5b680f9a370e408f806b3d5/20240624_SOLEWLH_Sintonizacin_Antioquia_LaGuayana_(3).mp4)

According to John and Yuli, young SOLE Ambassadors of the community, in order to connect to the Internet they have to walk almost 40 minutes from their homes, sometimes uphill, looking for a point where the signal can reach. In their own words, even in the best of cases the signal is weak and limited. Turn up the volume to hear them!

The situation began to change with the Sintonización Visits. During these meetings, we explained to the community how the Internet works and why connectivity is a challenge in their territory. We discussed the technical difficulties inherent to their mountainous terrain and the costs associated with infrastructure in such remote locations.

That day, the SOLE team and the community faced the challenge of installing an antenna and a rural kit, technology specially adapted for areas of difficult access. For hours, the connectivity solution was assembled and disassembled, and with the collaboration of the Ambassadors and other community members, strategic signal points were sought. After several attempts and a collective work of patience and effort, we were able to connect the router and antenna to a satellite signal source located about 30 meters away.

Thanks to this cacharrero effort, and to the fact that the Ambassadors did not give up until they achieved it, the community now has a community connection point. Although it is not the most stable, they can now access the Internet without the need to travel long distances.

 **Listen to Yuli's excitement as she declares herself an Internet Cacharrera in her community!**

[Tuning Visits in La Guayana, Anorí, Antioquia, (2024).](../../../¿Cómo mejoró el Internet en Anorí, Antioquia 1362bd68c5b680f9a370e408f806b3d5/20240624_SOLEWLH_Sintonizacin_Antioquia_LaGuayana_(166).6.mp4)

The best part is that the story doesn't end there: the Ambassadors have led SOLE sessions in their community throughout the year, where they answer great questions using the Internet as a group. In doing so, they have brought the use of technology and computers to their community, and opened doors for their neighbors to connect with the world.
