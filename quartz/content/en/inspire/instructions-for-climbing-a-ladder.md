## Instructions for climbing a ladder

- Julio Cortázar*

---

No one will have failed to notice that the floor often folds in such a way that one part rises at a right angle to the plane of the ground, and then the next part is placed parallel to this plane, to give way to a new perpendicular, a behavior that is repeated in a spiral or broken line up to extremely variable heights. By bending down and placing the left hand on one of the vertical parts, and the right on the corresponding horizontal, one is momentarily in possession of a step or rung. Each of these steps, formed as we can see by two elements, is placed slightly higher and forward than the previous one, a principle that gives meaning to the staircase, since any other combination will produce perhaps more beautiful or picturesque forms, but incapable of transporting from a ground floor to a first floor.

Stairs are climbed from the front, as climbing backward or sideways is particularly uncomfortable. The natural posture is to stand with arms hanging freely, head erect, though not so much so that the eyes no longer see the steps immediately above the one you are stepping on, and breathing slowly and regularly. To climb stairs, you begin by lifting that part of the body located on the bottom right, almost always wrapped in leather or suede, and which, with some exceptions, fits exactly on the step. Once this part, which we will call the foot for short, is placed on the first step, the equivalent part on the left (also called the foot, but not to be confused with the aforementioned foot) is picked up and, bringing it to the height of the foot, is made to continue until it is placed on the second step, whereupon the foot will rest on that step, and the foot will rest on the first step. (The first steps are always the most difficult, until the necessary coordination is acquired. The coincidence of names between foot and foot makes explanation difficult. Be especially careful not to lift foot and foot at the same time.)

Once you reach the second step in this way, simply repeat the movements alternately until you reach the top of the ladder. You can easily step off it with a light tap of your heel, which locks it in place until you descend.

- THE END*
