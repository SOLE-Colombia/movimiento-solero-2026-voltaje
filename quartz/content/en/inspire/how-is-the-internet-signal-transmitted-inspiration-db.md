The internet signal is transmitted **through physical infrastructure such as fiber optic and copper cables, as well as through radio waves (Wi-Fi) and, to a lesser extent, via satellite or mobile networks (4G/5G).** Data is converted into electrical or light pulses (bits) that travel through these media to their destination, using routers and other equipment to send and receive information.

**Guided Media (cable):**

- **Optical Fiber:** Transmits data as light pulses through thin glass wires.
- **Copper (Coaxial and Twisted Pair):** Uses metallic wires to carry electrical signals, as in ADSL connections.
- **Submarine Cables:** Most global internet traffic travels through fiber optic cables laid under the ocean.

**Wireless Media:**

- **Wi-Fi:** Uses radio waves to connect devices (like your phone or computer) to a router.
- **Mobile Networks (3G, 4G, 5G):** Signals are transmitted through antennas and cell towers to provide internet access for mobile devices.
- **Satellite:** Sends and receives signals to and from an orbiting satellite.

**How does the process work?**

1. **Conversion to bits:** When you send data (like an email), your computer converts it into pulses of electricity or light (bits).
2. **Transmission:** These bits travel through the physical infrastructure (cables, air, or space) to a network device.
3. **Routing:** Routers and other network equipment direct these bits along the most efficient path to their destination.
4. **Receiving:** At the receiving device, the bits are converted back into the original information that you can see or use.
