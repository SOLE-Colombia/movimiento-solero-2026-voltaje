## Welcome

Hi! I'm Manu and I'm not a professional designer, but that's what tinkering is all about, learning on your own. 

I love to design, draw and create. Collage making is one of my favorite hobbies. 

## What is it like to design an invitation, without being an expert?

I'm excited to teach you how to design an amazing invitation for your next SOLE or activity. At SOLE Colombia we have learned how to use [Canva](/canva-conceptorio), but you can design your invitation however you like, even by hand! you can do it!

## What is it for?

Many times we feel that we are not going to be able to make an invitation as beautiful as the ones made by others. Sometimes we don't have much manual skill, or we think that our creative ability is not enough.

But now you can change the chip and discover how to make a beautiful invitation! Whether by hand, or using technical resources, you can use different techniques that, for sure, will surprise you.

## What do you need?

- A computer, tablet or phone with Internet access (if you are going to use Canva).
- Colored paper, colored pencils, markers and pens (if you are going to do it by hand).
- Creativity.

## How to do it?

### Let's start with the analog - your invitation by hand or with traditional technique.

- **Color, make it look pretty**
    - Use colored paper or whatever you have on hand and start bringing your invitation to life using colored pencils, markers or pens.
    - Add creative details such as decorative borders, simple illustrations or symbols that represent the essence of your event. You can also think of colored boxes to highlight information that you think is valuable.
    - Play with different colors and shading techniques to make your design visually appealing and unique.
- **The text, say what you need to say**
    - Write the details of the event in a way that is easy to read. You can use your own handwriting or cut and paste printed letters such as those from newspapers, magazines, posters... you can even make some with play dough or other materials you have on hand!
    - Use the resources in the previous section to highlight essential information.
    - Be creative with the text and make sure it is clear to your guests. You can ask someone close to you for an opinion, as several pairs of eyes are always better than one.
    - Be careful not to overload it, otherwise people may get lost among so much information... remember, 'less is more'.
- **Review, it's important**
    - Review your design and add the final touches to perfect it, ask for feedback, and you are sure to get help from your trusted people.
    - Don't forget to **check the spelling of words very carefully**. A well-written message is well understood and does not give rise to confusion, remember that your goal is that people understand everything well and can attend what you are inviting them to.
    - If you want to make your invitation reach further, you can spread it digitally: scan your creation or simply take a high quality photo to distribute it by electronic messaging (mail, whatsapp, telegram...) or social networks.

## How do I know if this solution works?

You need to check if your invitation content and design work. The easiest thing to do is to get an outside opinion to make sure everything is in order and that you can send it out as soon as possible.

## Why might this solution work?

There are some key considerations when evaluating the design of your invitation, here are some questions that can guide your thinking:

- whether the information is clear enough (time, place...) and whether it is easy to find and understand, check the text, font and text/image configuration so that it is not too cluttered
- whether it is visually appealing, consistent with the theme of the event, captures attention and generates interest
- whether the style is aligned with the formality of the event and is appropriate for the target audience
- if it is easy to share, visualize, understand the steps to be taken or the call to action you are proposing.

All of these aspects work for you for your invitations, but you can also help others do theirs... and, even if you're not an expert now, you will be very soon!
