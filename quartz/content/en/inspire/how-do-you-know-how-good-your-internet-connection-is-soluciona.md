## Welcome

Hi, I'm Manuela. I'm the accountant of SOLE Colombia and, although I don't know much about technology, I've started to get a taste for tinkering and I'm learning a lot of things that keep getting me out of trouble.

## What is it?

Internet connection is not a ghost that accompanies us everywhere... or maybe it is a bit of a ghost! 

This is a way in which you can learn a little more about what the Internet is and how to know what is failing at any given moment, or how to improve your signal. 

## What is it good for?

Knowing how good your Internet connection is can give you clues to understand performance and troubleshoot possible problems. 

For example, it will let you know if the speed you contracted with your cell phone or internet service is what you are getting... or if you are being throttled.

You can identify the areas of your community, or your home, where you have the best signal. This way you can know where the movie you are watching will hang up, or where you need to position yourself to best receive someone's call.

You will be able to make decisions in case you need to reboot the router, or change the signal reception channel. Or determine if your modem needs to be changed. 

By optimizing the signal, you will have a faster and more stable connection to be able to do SOLE or access certain contents that require a better signal.

## What do you need?

- Your device
- Be connected to the Internet signal

## How to do it?

- **Step 1: Take your cell phone and see if you have a mobile signal.**
    
    Look at the top corner of your cell phone. There you will find some mobile signal bars: If you see one or more bars, the phone is receiving a signal and reading your [SIM card](/tarjeta-sim-sim-card-conceptorio).
    
- **Step 2: How good is the connection?**
    - [ ]  I have no mobile signal bars
    - [ ]  1 mobile signal bar
    - [ ]  2 mobile signal bars
    - [ ]  3 mobile signal bars
- **Step 3: What type of mobile connection do you have?**
    
    Look next to the mobile signal bars and you will see the type of signal you have. These letters indicate the speeds and technologies that carriers use to send you information.
    
- **Mobile connection types and what are they for?**
    
    **G:** Abbreviates a [**GPRS**](../Conceptorio 2602bd68c5b681c182aed46fccc7c4ea/Conceptorio 2602bd68c5b68191afe2e809f2a2154d/GPRS (Servicio General de Paquetes Vía Radio)%20GPRS%202602bd68c5b681c4b651ecfe3b559103.md) connection, **the slowest of all** technologies. It is a [**second generation or 2G**](/red-2g-2g-net-conceptorio) network used **to send text messages**.
    
    **E:** Abbreviates an[**EDGE**](/conexion-edge-edge-connection-conceptorio) [connection](/conexion-edge-edge-connection-conceptorio) , it is an improved version of GPRS, also called **2.5G**, and is used **to search the Internet**.
    
    **3G**: Called [**UMTS**](/umts-sistema-universal-de-telecomuniaciones-moviles-umts-universal-mobile-telecommunications-system-conceptorio), it is a **third generation or 3G** network, designed **for multimedia content**.
    
    **H**: It uses[**HSPA**](/concepto-concept-conceptorio) [technology](/concepto-concept-conceptorio) , an **improved 3G**, and is more than enough **to access social networks or listen to music on the Internet.**
    
    **H+**: It is an evolved [**HSPA**](/tecnologia-hspa-acceso-de-paquetes-de-alta-velocidad-hspa-technology-conceptorio) **to watch online videos or download them faster**.
    
    **4G**: It is the technology also called [**LTE**](/conexion-4g-o-lte-lte-or-4g-connection-conceptorio), it is considered as the **fourth generation or 4G**. The speed of this network **reaches 100 Mbps download and 50 Mbps upload,** what we call **broadband,** very fast!
    
    **5G**: It is the fastest technology of all, for now, and it is up to **100 times faster than 4G**. You need a phone that supports 5G to use it.
    
    - [ ]  **G:** text messaging only
    - [ ]  E: browsing web pages
    - [ ]  3G, H, H+: social networking, music and videos
    - [ ]  4G, LTE: video calls and HD videos

## How do you know if this solution works?

If you are able to get as far as the speed test result and know how fast your Internet is going, you are on the right track. You already have the information about the characteristics of your connection.

## Why would this solution work?

Now, you can reflect, is the contracted speed the same as the one I have on my device? If not, maybe it is time to ask yourself, is the speed I have enough for what I need it for? 

These practical, but at the same time somewhat philosophical, questions can give you arguments to make more conscious decisions, maybe it is time to switch to a cheaper plan or complain to your service provider. 

Maybe you can also make decisions about taking up a collection with your neighbors to pay for an internet for everyone (which would be much cheaper), or analyze your surroundings to look for places that have good internet and spend some time a day there.
