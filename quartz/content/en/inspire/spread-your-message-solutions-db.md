# Reach the hearts of the people your message touches

## Welcome

Good morning! This is Manu. Today I want to share with you some powerful techniques you can use to get your message to more people and generate a positive impact in your community.

## What is spreading your message?

When it comes to spreading [a message](/crea-un-mensaje-potente-solutions-db) that really matters to us, we want to make sure that it reaches as many people as possible, and that those people respond to our call. 

In this solution, I'll share some strategies with you so you can test which medium is the best for getting your message across.  

## What is it good for?

Knowing how to best disseminate your message will allow you to reach [your audience](/descubre-a-tu-audiencia-solutions-db) more efficiently. You will be able to reach a wider audience, more specialized in what you want to tell them, you will be able to inform, spread, infect... in the best possible way! 

Whether others can appreciate the value and importance of what you say is related to the way you disseminate it, so, if you want to 'reach better' and make more strategic decisions, you need to 

## **What do you need?**

- Colored paper and/or cardstock.
- Colored pencils, markers and pens.
- Computer or cell phone with Internet access to send messages via WhatsApp.
- Comfortable shoes to personally connect with the community.
- A good dose of enthusiasm and authenticity.

## How to do it?

- **Moment 0: Define what you want to tell and what you want your audience to do.**
    
    To define your message in detail, you can review the solution [**Create a powerful message**](/crea-un-mensaje-potente-solutions-db), where you can define exactly what you want to spread and what is the purpose of your communication.
    
    Now, to know if your message is being powerful, you need to include in your message a key aspect: the call to action, or what is called in communication Call To Action. This refers to the action you are asking the person to take, once they have received your message, and which will confirm to you that this is indeed the case. 
    
    For example, a call to action might be "sign up for the list below". As soon as you see a number of names written down, you will know that your message has been well received. In digital media, you can use reactions (emojis, comments) to find out if people are seeing your content, or design some other way to get people to interact with you and confirm that your message has reached them.
    
- **Moment 1: First of all, who is your target audience, who do you want to address?**
    
    You need to think about [your audience](https://www.notion.so/e00a6e1836c643c3b2995d5bf43be1bf?pvs=21) before deciding on the channel you are going to use to deliver your message. You can adapt to different media, the important thing is that the message reaches the person you want to reach.
    
    Keep in mind that, if you have partnered with a place that convenes (see the solution [¿Cómo aliarse con lugares que convocan?](/como-aliarse-con-lugares-que-convocan-solutions-db)), it may be that your partner already knows very well the people it knows how to convene. If so, you can let your ally advise you and learn from their way of doing things!
    
    If you already know who you want to invite or reach with your message, a good tactic is to use the media most used by those people.
    
    
💡  For example, if your target audience is young people between 18 and 25 years old interested in technology, platforms like WhatsApp and other Social Networks could be effective channels to reach them.

    
    Activity: 
    
    1. Describe on a sheet of paper your target audience and ask yourself: what do the people you want to reach do? how old are they? what do they like to do? 
    2. Draw the media these people would normally use: do they use Social Networks? do they read newspapers? do they listen to community radio? 
    3. Underline in the list which are the winning media.

- **Moment 2: Second, define the channel, how are you going to reach them?**
    - **The digital broadcast channels.**
        
        WhatsApp, Instagram, email, Facebook, Phone, it doesn't matter which one. What matters is that they are accessible media for your audience, that is, the people you want to call, or to whom you want to reach.
        
        In Colombia WhatsApp is widely used to send messages. I invite you to exploit all the resources it has: send voice notes, messages, photos, make a group, you can even create polls to decide what time is best for all your guests to participate.

    - **Analogous dissemination channels**
        
        
        **Create billboards and flyers**
        
        Stick billboards, flyers, flyers, that transmit your message in strategic places. Go to places where your community gathers such as local stores or community centers. 
        
        You can also knock on your neighbors' doors and hand deliver the [invitation](https://www.notion.so/1803914493dc4bf5811aac25bf2bbb8b?pvs=21).

        **Get out on the street**
        
        Time to put on your comfiest shoes and go out and conquer the community! 
        
        Go to the places where the people of your community, or their leaders, gather.... Give yourself the opportunity to jump right in and talk to strangers, it's your time to shine!
        
        When you approach people, tell them why your activity is so important.
        
        Listen carefully and show genuine interest in what they say. Make authentic connections and make new friends while sharing your passion. 
        
        Every conversation is an opportunity to learn and make the impact of your activity greater!

- **Moment 3: Reach out to others for help, there is strength in numbers.**
    
    By joining forces with others, we multiply our impact. Join with your peers, community leaders, friends and family who share your values and goals. Invite them to join in and tell them about your message so they can replicate it, ensuring that your activity is on everyone's lips.
    
    Seek support from organizations, community radio stations, local media, schools or community action boards committed to your cause or activity. Approach their spaces, share the importance of your activity and the details of your call, such as dates and times, clearly. They will surely be able to help you replicate the call.
    
    *Remember, word of mouth is key to expand your impact; work as a team and make your message reach more people!*
    
- **Moment 4: Receive the responses and evaluate the success of your campaign.**
    
    Remember earlier we talked about the *call to action*? That is, are the people to whom you addressed your message doing what you asked them to do? Bravo! So, you did get your message out. 
    
    Evaluate the reception and impact of your message - everything counts! For example, the hearts given to your message on WhatsApp, the comments, or the messages you received by listening to the direct response of someone in your community.
    

## How do you know this solution works?

It is important that you monitor the way your message reaches your audience. To know this, sometimes it is enough to ask how they felt about it, if it reached them clearly enough, if they felt that the way you executed the dissemination was relevant and sufficient.

There are some indicators with which you can evaluate the adequacy of a message's dissemination: 

- whether you have been able to deliver it and someone has received it
- whether any of the calls to action have had an effect
- whether other people received the message, even if they were not the ones you initially addressed it to
- if you received good feedback

## Why might this solution work?

If you're not getting the responses you expect, or something in the outreach doesn't seem to be working well, it's time to check. 

Make sure your message has relevant content:

- has relevant, targeted content for your audience (below are some important considerations)
- is focused on a target audience
- it is a quality written message, check grammar, spelling and syntax, and that it is easy to understand
- the way you send it is aligned with your audience (time of day, day of the week, medium you are sending it through...)
- it is important to be respectful, both with people's data and with the way and the moment in which we communicate things, keep this in mind.

Communication is a living process, you can revise it as many times as you want and you will get better and better at it. The more you practice, the better and more easily you will be able to create powerful messages and spread them in a more efficient and powerful way.
