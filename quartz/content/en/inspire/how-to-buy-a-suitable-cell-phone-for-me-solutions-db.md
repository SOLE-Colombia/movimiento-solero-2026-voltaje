# Buying a cell phone that fits your capabilities and needs

![Imagen](/assets/images/¿Cómo comprar un celular adecuado para mí 12a2bd68c5b6807f99afed36f9b83c06/Comprar_un_celular00-800w.webp)

## Welcome

Hi I'm Nicolas, and like you, I've been progressively learning how to improve my connectivity to do SOLE. I'm not an expert on the subject, but through internet searches, tutorials and, above all, trial and error, I've learned a lot and have put together functional solutions. 

## What is buying the right cell phone for you?

In this particular solution we will learn how to buy a cell phone according to your possibilities and needs. Below you will find a series of information, criteria and tips that try to be as clear as possible so you can feel accompanied in making a decision on what cell phone you can buy. The estimated reading time is about 12 minutes and consists of 10 items, butdo not panic, you can read these items with the help of others. Remember that in SOLE Voltage we want to promote the collective use of the Internet. 

## What is it for?

In this solution you will find a few practical tips to help you make a much more informed decision about which cell phones are right for you.

## **Before we start, some ethical issues!**

At SOLE Voltage we talk from supporting people clarify the ethical implication of a decision by problematizing the circumstance that leads you to make that decision. This solution starts from the assumption that you want to buy a cell phone, but **if you really need it,** this is not always the best option for you. Sometimes you have an old cell phone that a family member can give you or, for different reasons, you prefer not to have a cell phone and it is a better option for you to have a computer. If, definitely after making a sweep of your options you consider that you need to buy a cell phone, this is the right place for you! **Here is some advice on what to consider when buying a cell phone.**

## **What do you need?**

- Save money for the purchase.
- Internet access.
- A reliable store or dealer.
- Curiosity.

## **How to do it?**

- **Moment 0: How to know if I need to buy a cell phone?**
    
    One of the characteristics of our current times is the great development of international and national markets, our cell phones are made in China and we can buy them in Colombia, we can go to the market and buy rice brought from Brazil. In addition, we find disproportionate consumption options, we can buy white rice, yellow rice, bulk rice, lemon flavored rice, etc.. There are endless options to become consumers of products that, many times, we do not need, so we end up buying non-nutritious food, and we fall into one more expense that does not satisfy the main function of food, to nourish us; in the same way, we fill ourselves with endless merchandise at home that ends up piled up or discarded because, in the end, it was not useful for anything. This is why we want to promote **responsible consumption.** In the case of a cell phone, we suggest you ask yourself the **following questions** to find out if it is a responsible purchase:
    
    - Do you need to buy a cell phone or do you have other options to access the internet and make calls?
    - Can you buy another option that can supplement your internet connection, calls and can be paid for by several people?
    - If you buy a cell phone will you really improve your quality of life or will it get worse? For example, are you now addicted to watching content or social networks or are you going to be more stressed every month thinking about how to pay for this new expense?
    - Finally, is this new consumption really a priority for you?
- **Moment 1: Why do I need a cell phone?**
    
    Just like a car, **the cell phone we choose should fit our main needs.** It doesn't make much sense to buy a 4x4 truck if you are going to be driving around a city with paved roads.
    
    Now, think about what will be the most important activities and in which you will invest more time in your cell phone: ***do you want to take a lot of pictures of your region?*** then you should pay attention to the characteristics of the camera; **are you going to be outside the house in the middle of the countryside all day, or are you going to use your phone as a modem to share your internet with other people participating in a SOLE?** A good battery would be essential; **do you want to use the internet to learn in a group?** a big screen with good definition is important; **do you want to play a video game with high definition?** a good processor is a must; **do you need to work with several applications at the same time?** look at the RAM memory; **do you need to have many applications and large files?** look at the internal storage.
    
    Now that you have decided whether to buy a cell phone, I will make an introduction taking my case as an example: 
    
    
💡 At the beginning of 2024 I threw my cell phone away when I got out of a cab, because of my jobs and contact with my family and friends it is important for me to have a cell phone. Some time ago I decided that I will not invest more than $ 1'000,000 COP in this tool. About 5 years ago I met a new brand that was emerging, it had good prices and had very good references from friends and users on the internet, this brand is Xiaomi, my last two cell phones had been of this brand, each one was worth about $ 700,000 COP. However, I searched the internet for references and I saw that there is a new brand that is coming and is economical, Tecno, I looked at several references and saw that it is the best selling brand currently by free market, so I looked for a cell phone with similar characteristics to the previous ones, 250 Gb of internal memory, a decent processor, a battery of more than 4000 mA / h, a large screen and at least 8 Gb of RAM. These features are compatible with my work and personal use, I do not take professional photos, nor was I looking for it to play video games. Finally I found a cell phone of this brand for $ 475,000 COP, this cell phone I bought was a Tecno Spark 20 Dual Sim 256 Gb. So far I can say that it has been an excellent purchase!

    Now it's your turn to identify what you need the phone for!
    
- **Moment 2: Set a budget**
    
    
    First of all, **define what your budget will be**, for this you can do the math of how many days of work you are willing to invest for this device. Do not get into debt for a cell phone, many times we are offered 1 or 2 year financing, and the cell phone is damaged or stolen before we finish paying for it.
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/Antena 3 4G 3 4G Antenna 22b2bd68c5b68076a24ccc4e1d6ff4af/image-800w.webp)
    

Once you are clear about the above, you will know what features you should pay more attention to.

- **Camera**
    
    If you love taking pictures, here are some aspects that are central to detailing the camera of your device:
    
    **Resolution**
    
    This is what we commonly know as **megapixels**, although it is very important it is not all we should look at when evaluating a camera. The more megapixels it has, the more detail you will see in the images. However, you must have a limit. The standard might be a **12 MP** camera, because if it has good features and excellent image processing, you will get great quality photos. If you start having higher resolution photos the files will be very heavy and will fill up the internal storage of your cell phone quickly.
    
    **Sensor and lenses**
    
    ![lente.jpg](../../../../../Soluciona 1292bd68c5b681c6b5b5cec1700cc864/Solutions DB 1292bd68c5b681d1b87ccbb89a1b1d39/¿Cómo comprar un celular adecuado para mí 12a2bd68c5b6807f99afed36f9b83c06/lente.jpg)
    
    **Aperture**
    
    The lens aperture will be fundamental for a good photo, this characteristic **defines how much light the camera can capture.** It is identified by the **letter f followed by a number**, for example, f1.9 or f 2.0. The lower this number, the more light the camera will capture. Therefore, **it is important that it is low**, because with a higher light input, the images will have more detail.
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/¿Cómo comprar un celular adecuado para mí 12a2bd68c5b6807f99afed36f9b83c06/Megapixel_comparison-400w.webp)
    
    A good cell phone camera must have a quality sensor, as well as **several lenses**. The latter, in fact, has already become a standard. When combined, they make it possible to obtain better images. One of the most popular lenses is the wide-angle lens. Thanks to this, much more information is incorporated into the photos and it provides a visual angle of up to 120 degrees, perfect for group photos or indoors where there is not much space.
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/¿Cómo comprar un celular adecuado para mí 12a2bd68c5b6807f99afed36f9b83c06/apertura-400w.webp)
    
- **Screen**
    
    A screen will define whether our eyes get tired more or less quickly, whether or not we will see a certain degree of detail or how quickly the battery will be consumed.
    
    When you have clear that your cell phone must be a device to be used in a group, as it happens when we do SOLE, this feature becomes important, since it is not so easy for several people to read on a small screen.
    
    **Screen size**
    
    The size of cell phone screens has increased over time, although in recent years they have not grown much, since they lose their portability function in the pocket. We could say that most cell phones are between 3.5" and 6" (this measures the diagonal of this, ie from one upper corner to the opposite lower corner). It really depends on your taste, do you want a large screen that allows you to watch a video from a distance or do you prefer a small screen so that your cell phone does not attract so much attention?
    
    ![Tamaño pantalla.png](../../../../../Soluciona 1292bd68c5b681c6b5b5cec1700cc864/Solutions DB 1292bd68c5b681d1b87ccbb89a1b1d39/¿Cómo comprar un celular adecuado para mí 12a2bd68c5b6807f99afed36f9b83c06/Tamano_pantalla.png)
    
    **Screen technology**
    
    **Full HD technology** is more recent than **HD** and the number of pixels is higher compared to HD which works with **1,280 x 720 pixels**. Full HD gives a much more realistic look to the image. The latter technology is only coming in the latest cell phones with large panels, as it does not make much sense to have high resolution on small screens.
    
    **Screen type**
    
    There are different types of screens, but in the current market two are the most popular: LCD technology, also known as inorganic, and OLED technology, known as organic.
    
    - **LCD:** These are backlit liquid crystal displays that usually cover the entire panel. In the past they were the most popular, but they are increasingly produced in smaller quantities. As they are backlit, they will consume more energy, but for this reason they look good in direct sunlight.
    - **OLED:** These screens differ from LCDs because their materials allow them to generate light by themselves when electricity is applied, this allows them to turn off or turn on each pixel independently, so they **consume less energy.** In addition, they end up being **brighter and have better black color definition.**
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/¿Cómo comprar un celular adecuado para mí 12a2bd68c5b6807f99afed36f9b83c06/tipo_de_pantalla-800w.webp)
    
- **Internal storage**
    
    
    The internal storage defines the amount of files we can store on our cell phone, it is like the hard drive on a computer. This will determine how many programs you can have installed at a time, whether or not you can store all types of files. At this time we recommend buying a cell phone with at least 64 GB of internal memory. Also, see if you have the option to receive microSD cards to expand the capacity in the future.
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/Conexión cable Ethernet Ethernet cable connection 22b2bd68c5b680fa90ceecc23bea76db/image 1-275w.webp)
    
    .
    
- **RAM memory**
    
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/A pocket Wikipedia in your RaspberryPi 1a42bd68c5b680289571fc002238a69a/image 2-800w.webp)
    
    This is the temporary memory of the cell phone, it helps in the performance and gives a clue of how fast the applications open and how fast the cell phone flows. We recommend a **minimum of 4GB of RAM.**
    
- **Processor**
    
    
    The processor is the **brain**, it is the one that is always awake and receives instructions. The more cores the processor has, the better performance it will have, although it will also depend on the brand. Here, the best option is to look at the name of the processor used by the cell phone that catches your attention and do a little research on your own. **Hopefully it was not released more than 4 years ago.**
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/A pocket Wikipedia in your RaspberryPi 1a42bd68c5b680289571fc002238a69a/image 3-800w.webp)
    
- **Battery**
    
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/A pocket Wikipedia in your RaspberryPi 1a42bd68c5b680289571fc002238a69a/image 4-800w.webp)
    
    It is one of the most important components of the cell phone, since it will condition its use. Autonomy, charging time and battery life are parameters that will affect us. Its capacity is indicated by the number of milliamps per hour (mAh) it can provide to an electronic circuit. Thus, a capacity of 3,000 mAh indicates that the battery is capable of delivering a current of 3,000 mAh for one hour before it is exhausted. We recommend that with the current consumption of a cell phone the minimum capacity is 4000 mA/h.

## **Don't be fooled**

It is important that you constantly check emerging brands, usually there are brands with little advertising, very good features and a low price. However, many times out of habit and experience we continue to buy the same brand as the last cell phone. For example, my best friend has been using the Motorola brand since college, I think that although it is a good brand, it has very high costs, and I prefer new cheaper brands that have good references to save some money. 

Another important element has to do with the warranty, when you live in a central city is easy if something happens with the cell phone there is a branch of all brands available in case you have to use the warranty, but in less central cities, for example Bucaramanga, the brand of my current cell phone "Tecno" has no office so if you live there you would have to send the phone to Bogota, pay more money and wait longer to get it back, but possibly in this city if there is a center of attention of more popular brands such as Motorola.

## **How much is enough?**

This question is difficult to answer, but I think we can rephrase it as follows: **Does the option you chose meet the need and intention you projected at the beginning?** In the previous example I showed you how I consider that the cell phone I currently have was a very good choice for my lifestyle. This type of reasoning is what we want you to do when making a decision about the option you are going to buy.
