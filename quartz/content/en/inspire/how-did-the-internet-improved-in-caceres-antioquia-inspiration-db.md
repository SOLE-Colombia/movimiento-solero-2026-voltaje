In the Altos del Tigre community, in the municipality of Cáceres, five community leaders are making something almost impossible a reality: improving internet access in their community.

Yadiris Vaquero, Luz Cenith Acevedo, Kenia Yepes, Blanca Ofir Moreno, and David Ojeda, SOLE Ambassadors from their community, have been working for some time to ensure that internet access ceases to be a hidden treasure in their area. Because yes, in Cáceres, as in many places throughout Colombia, connection has always been a luxury.

This year, however, something changed. Starting with the Tuning Visit back in May, the Ambassadors and other community members took the opportunity to learn together how the internet works and why, specifically in Cáceres, getting a signal was a challenge. The answer? Basically, because there are no antennas for miles around and the telecommunications infrastructure is minimal.

But this didn't discourage them. On the contrary, they got to work. They assessed the situation, looked for solutions, and, with the program's support, found the ideal option for them: a Jangala device. This small but powerful device makes it possible to connect communities in hard-to-reach areas, and for the residents of Cáceres, it was like a gateway to a new world.

Thus, thanks to Jangala and the efforts of the SOLE Ambassadors, and all the people in the community who joined together to install this solution, Cáceres now has Internet access at a community access point.

And for many, this achievement means much more than "being connected": it's an opportunity to learn, to be informed, to stay up-to-date. Children, young people, and adults can now access information, participate in SOLE sessions, and, as they say, "answer those big questions that previously seemed like something out of this world."

The work of Yadiris, Luz Cenith, Kenia, Blanca Ofir, and David Ojeda has marked a turning point in Cáceres. Today, thanks to their dedication and Jangala, the community not only has the Internet, but is transforming how it uses it, discovering, sharing, and creating many possibilities for their community.
