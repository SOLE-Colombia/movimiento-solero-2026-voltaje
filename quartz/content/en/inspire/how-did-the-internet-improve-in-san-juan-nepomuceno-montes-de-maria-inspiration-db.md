The following excerpts are a compilation of testimonies, images, and scenes about how group internet use in the municipality of **San Juan Nepomuceno** is transforming internet access for neighboring communities, such as San José del Peñón and La Haya.

### First Stop

[Festivales SOLE, Montes de María, (2024).](../../../How did the internet improve in San Juan Nepomucen 2602bd68c5b680d8be2fc8497ed318f4/WhatsApp_Video_2024-10-31_at_10.58.46(1).mp4)

Festivales SOLE, Montes de María, (2024).

To reach San José del Peñón, you must cross seven rivers. You cross your fingers to avoid the rain, and you marvel at the majestic beauty of this land. It's no coincidence that, upon arrival, a large sign proudly welcomes you: "San José del Peñón, magical, fertile, and powerful land.”

The geography of San José del Peñón, with its mountains and dense vegetation, prevents signals from nearby antennas from reaching the area. Installing a community connectivity solution was a challenge that required the efforts of the entire community. With the support and collective work, we managed to install, **against all odds**, our Rural Kit: a Yagi antenna and a router that now make it possible for the community to gather periodically to practice SOLE.

To tinker, you don't need to be an expert or have experience building networks, antennas, or cables. Like Don Rafael Antonio, you too can be encouraged to collaborate with others to create a common good.

Currently, the community of San José del Peñón, together with its community, has continued to do SOLE on its own, using the Internet as a group to investigate Big Questions such as: *Why is water wet? Why don't humans use 100% of our brains? Why is there fresh water and salt water? Why are children born without teeth?*

### Second Stop:

Since 2023, we've built a super-close relationship with the La Haya community. This year, one of the SOLE Ambassadors from there joined as a SOLE Explorer to help us bring a connectivity solution she already knew to San José del Peñón. Mariana Fernández was our indispensable support in training new ambassadors in her municipality and successfully installing the connectivity solution in this new SOLE community.

Turn up the volume to hear what this experience was like in her own words, and how using the internet as a group has transformed her territory:

[Mariana Fernández, Visitas de Sintonización, Montes de María, (2024).](../../../How did the internet improve in San Juan Nepomucen 2602bd68c5b680d8be2fc8497ed318f4/20240711_SOLEWLH_Sintonizacin_MM_SanJuandeNepomuceno_(15).2.mp4)

Mariana Fernández, Visitas de Sintonización, Montes de María, (2024).

Thanks to community collaboration and the implementation of solutions like the Rural Kit, San José del Peñón and La Haya now have community internet access, facilitating both learning and cultural exchange in the Montes de María.
