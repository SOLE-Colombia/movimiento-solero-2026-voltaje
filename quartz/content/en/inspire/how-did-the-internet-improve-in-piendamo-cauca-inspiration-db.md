The USAID and IOM *Threading Lives and Hope* program brought SOLE Colombia to El Agrado. Together, we have worked to bridge the digital divide by bringing internet access to isolated communities, giving connection a purpose: using the internet together to learn in a self-organized and autonomous way. Since 2023, Piendamó, Cauca, has been the center of this story of change.

**Alexander García, the Junk Merchant Who Builds Bridges**

It was in El Agrado that we met Alexander García, a young community leader with a curious and committed spirit. From the beginning, Alexander was enthusiastic about technology, and especially the internet. With SOLE's support, we installed a connectivity solution and some computers in the community shelter, which soon became the learning and meeting center for his community.

## The Journey to the Misak Pisicatau Reservation

Alexander's curiosity to understand how routers, antennas, and networks work quickly led him to become an expert. In a matter of months, he went from someone who didn't care about technology to a "tinkerer." This newly acquired skill has made him a bridge between his community and other neighboring communities, sharing his knowledge with others.

In 2024, Alexander joined us on a new mission: to bring internet to a neighboring Indigenous community in the Misak Pisicatau Reservation. Upon arrival, we conducted speed and signal tests, and it was Alexander who quickly discovered that no antenna could provide a stable connection in that mountainous region.

With his technical skills and passion for teaching, Alexander was the first to motivate the residents of the reservation to explore the possibilities of the internet and join the SOLE movement. With his support, we installed computers in the community shelter, and Alexander encouraged them to continue using Self-Organized Learning in their community. Thanks to his encouragement, the Misak Pisicatau community has now created its own website, a space where they share their traditions, culture, and knowledge with the world.

Alexander has transformed not only his own knowledge, but also that of entire communities. Now, on the Misak Pisicatau Reservation, fear of technology has become a tool to preserve their identity, share their cultural heritage, and jointly address big questions like *Why don't Big Data and Artificial Intelligence have an ethnic focus?*
