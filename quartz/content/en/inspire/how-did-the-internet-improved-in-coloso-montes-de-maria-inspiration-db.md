La Estación and the La Esmeralda Indigenous Council are just a 15-minute motorcycle ride from each other. Both are neighboring communities in the Colombian municipality of Colosó, in the department of Sucre. Until recently, neither of these communities had a public space for group internet access. However, since 2023, they have ventured to use the connectivity solutions we installed together in their territory to promote Self-Organized Learning.

No one better than Mónica Vega, one of La Estación's Star Ambassadors, to share the story of how these communities are transforming their access to information, connection, and education. Are you ready to listen? Don't forget to turn up the volume!

Mónica Vega, en Visitas de Sintonización, Montes de María, (2024).

Did you hear her? It's incredible how her community has used Jangala and internet access to strengthen ties among its members. But Monica's story doesn't end there. This year, she was inspired to visit her neighboring community, La Esmeralda, to encourage more people to tinker with technology.

Even though Jangala wasn't the right solution for this community, Monica and Gabriel, being the excellent "tinkerers" that they are, got to work, and nothing stopped them. Together, we explored other connectivity options and eventually installed an omnidirectional antenna and a router, with Monica's help. Now, the La Esmeralda Ambassadors are empowering their neighbors to use the internet as a group.

[Gabriel, en Visitas de Sintonización, Montes de María, (2024).](../../../¿Cómo mejoró el Internet en Colosó, Montes de Marí 1302bd68c5b680259bbffb361b4d5867/20240709_SOLEWLH_Sintonizacin_MM_Colos_(9).mp4)

Gabriel, en Visitas de Sintonización, Montes de María, (2024).

In addition, the community has created its own website to tell the world about its products, traditions, services, territory, history, and customs. The community Internet is now in their hands!
