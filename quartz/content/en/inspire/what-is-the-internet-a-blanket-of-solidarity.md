When you think of the Internet, what's the first thing that comes to mind?

If we think of libraries as a repository of human knowledge written in books; that today computers hold that information, and much more is stored as zeros and ones in their memory; then we could imagine the Internet as the sum of all the information created and stored on all the computers on the planet, which are connected through a network of cables and wireless signals and can exchange that information with each other.

The Internet is much more than that, but in short: it's a large decentralized network of computers that communicate with each other.

There are other communication networks in the world, for example, postal mail, telephone, radio, and television. A big difference between these and the Internet is that on the Internet, people can communicate in both directions and exchange digital information very quickly—at the speed of light!
