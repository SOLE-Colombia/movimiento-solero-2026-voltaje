KM15 is the geographical point on the road that leads to the Puerto Valdivia district from the municipal seat. It is also the name of the community located there. Life in *Km15—*kilometer 15, as it sounds in Spanish—runs alongside the highway that connects it to the rest of the world… or not. If its geographical location is a metaphor for its digital situation: in many places the internet is an everyday occurrence, there it feels distant.

Community internet access was a dream for its inhabitants. Most relied on expensive data plans on their cell phones or satellite internet, options that are not always affordable and that limit their possibilities.

As a result of their exposure to new ways of using the internet, the women of KM15, who had already experienced the power of using the internet in groups, were drivers of change through the USAID-run Spinning Lives and Hope program implemented by IOM and SOLE Colombia.

Their commitment to learning led them to tinker with their ideas to transform this reality. Accompanied by SOLE Colombia, and guided by their enthusiasm and dedication, we were able to install a Jangala device in the community booth: a space that, from one moment to the next, became the connection hub for KM15.

The donation of several computers completed the toolkit with which these Ambassadors can now lead SOLE sessions in their community, bringing their neighbors closer to learning, technology, and a world full of possibilities.

Today, the community booth is not only a meeting place, but also a place to share the internet and manage resources communally.

The story doesn't end there. The Ambassadors now understand how the Internet works, why it's important to manage it, and the difference in internet usage that can occur between watching a movie or sending a text message. They are now experts using computers and the Internet in groups.
