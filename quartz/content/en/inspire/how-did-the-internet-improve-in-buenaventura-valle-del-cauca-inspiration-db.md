This story begins in 2023, when we arrived in Calle Larga, in Buenaventura. There we were, with our rural connectivity kit. The idea was simple: to give them a better Internet connection so they could use it in the SOLE (Self-Organized Learning Spaces) sessions.  That's when we met her:

[Rosa Helena Mina, Tuning Visits in La Delfina, Buenaventura, (2024).](../../../How did the Internet improve in Buenaventura, Vall 25c2bd68c5b680389d77d3f1869a5e2e/20240611_SOLEWLH_Sintonizacin_LaDelfina87.3.mp4)

Rosa Helena Mina, Tuning Visits in La Delfina, Buenaventura, (2024).

Over time, Rosa Helena became the "cacharrera" and SOLE Ambassador of the community, always willing to learn and help others to solve any connection problem that arose. Here in this video she is telling us how, little by little, she was learning and discovering what she had to do to have a more stable Internet connection.

Rosa Helena got fully involved in the project, with her curiosity and desire to learn, she led SOLE sessions. In Calle Larga, they no longer only have Internet, but a space for young people and adults to learn using the Internet in a group.

It didn't stop there. In 2024, Rosa Helena was encouraged to visit a neighboring community, La Delfina, to share what she had learned. She arrived there with all her enthusiasm, ready to help them install their own Internet and to motivate them to tinker, so that they themselves could keep it working. And that's where she met Jefferson.

Jefferson is one of those young people who is not afraid to mess with cables, routers, antennas and anything that smells of technology. With Rosa Helena's help, he also joined the hacking patch and became a new SOLE leader in La Delfina. Imagine the excitement: your own Internet and someone who knows how to maintain it!

To tinker with the Internet you don't need to be an expert, just curious and eager to solve a situation. This year Jefferson has led SOLE sessions in his community, bringing them closer and closer to the use of technology and improving Internet access. So much so that, at the SOLE Festivals, they made their own web page to tell the whole world about their community. 

Thanks to the enthusiasm of Rosa Helena and Jefferson, now in La Delfina they not only have Internet, but a tool to create and share their own world. At the last SOLE Festival, they got together to create their own web page and now they share their history, their customs and even what they have learned in the SOLE sessions with other communities and the whole world.
