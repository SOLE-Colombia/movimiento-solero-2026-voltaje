Think about when you go to a restaurant and order a cup of coffee. You, as a **customer**, ask or *consult* the waiter, and he goes to the kitchen and comes back some time later and **serves** you the cup of coffee ready, like a good **server.** You're not quite clear on how he prepared it, but you know it's a cup of coffee when you taste it. The Internet works in a similar way. Let's see.

![SOLE Colombia, 2024.](../../../¿Cómo funciona el Internet 1362bd68c5b680c3b903ead1b7123665/20241101_Grafico__Como_funciona_el_Internet.png)

SOLE Colombia, 2024.

When you connect to the Internet and you have a question, what you do is to *consult it* from a cell phone or computer and you are communicating with many computers of other people or companies that store what you want to find. 

The information you want to *consult* can be a web page, a video, a photo, a video call, a file or a message.  These are some of the digital file **formats** that are sent over the Internet

The cell phone, computer or tablet from which you connect to the Internet is called **client.** 

The computer where the information you are consulting is stored is called the **server**.

## How does the information travel through the Internet?

In order for the information sent by a **server** to reach your home, neighborhood, village or community, the **client** you use connects to the Internet. Let's take, for example, your cell phone somewhere in Colombia and the response is on a server in Europe. This is the great adventure that your question takes.

![Imagen](/assets/images/¿Cómo funciona el Internet 1362bd68c5b680c3b903ead1b7123665/20241101_Grafico__como_viaja_tu_pregunta_por_internet-800w.webp)

How does your question travel through the Internet? SOLE Colombia, 2024.

Your cell phone connects by electromagnetic or wireless waves to one or more cell phone towers. Your question travels in the form of zeros "0" and ones "1" to one of those towers and down a cable that goes underground to some central wired location. It is then redirected to a seaport where that cable connects to a submarine cable. This goes under the ocean to the other continent and then out of the water to another cable point that carries your question to the server where your answer is located. The server receives your question and responds with the information it has stored - also in zeros "0" and ones "1" - and sends it back along the same route to your phone. And all this happens in a matter of milliseconds! It's amazing, don't you think?

It should be noted that submarine cables allow us to transmit data in order for telecommunications services to exist. The reason why information travels so fast is because the cables are made of fiber optics, i.e. glass. This means that the information, the zeros "0" and "1" are sent in the form of light and light is the fastest traveling thing in the entire universe.
