There's a question I get asked a lot when I share what a Grandma does at a SOLE: *What happens if there's no connection?* The answer is easy to explain, but not easy to understand. I always say: one solves it! And people look at me wondering, "But if it's a video call and there's no connection, *what's the point of being the Grandma at SOLE?"*

What really happens is that **one realizes that communication and presence don't need many embellishments** to happen.

Obviously, if the SOLE has a super good connection, everything is very organic. People chat, we all hear each other, we can interact easily, we converse and do incredible searches... it feels easy, pleasant, and, when it's over, the heart feels full and happy.

When those conditions aren't met and the connection is difficult, the image is pixelated, the sound is choppy, we don't even hear the Big Question, or even the video call chat doesn't seem to work synchronously. Frustration invades us, we don't understand what's happening, we want to hang up and continue with our commitments, because *this doesn't make any sense*... This feeling is multiplied if it's one of our first connections, if we've just started on La Nube de Abuelitas.

At the same time, on the other side, there's a community that can't hear us either; they see us in a blur. There are too many of them. The computer screen where Grandma is is small, and the sound coming out of the speaker is too low. Maybe it's hot, or it's after lunch. During the Attunement Visits, for example, it's the first time the communities have done SOLE, and they don't really understand what's going on, how they're going to find answers online, how to use the computer, what they need to do at each moment, or who that person is trying to talk to them from who knows where.

From experience, I can tell you: **the power of the internet is like the power of water**... it seeps in everywhere! It seeps in everywhere! And that's when a message, whatever it may be, from the other side of the world tells you, "**I'm with you,** that question you're asking is great, I'm searching and I found (...) congratulations on that research, I'll see you at the next SOLE!" And that's when the atmosphere fills with joy, letting us all know that we came here to learn, that we are TOGETHER. And that TOGETHER takes on a much greater and more powerful meaning.

From here, we thank everyone who didn't give up, those who sent a question or answer via WhatsApp, those who voted during the poll, and all those who followed the action minute by minute... THANK YOU to all of you!

A la vez, del otro lado, hay una comunidad que tampoco nos oye, nos ve borrosas, son muchos, la pantalla del computador donde está la Abuelita es pequeña, el sonido que sale por el parlante está muy bajito. Quizás hace calor o es después del almuerzo. En las Visitas de Sintonización, por ejemplo, es la primera vez que las comunidades hacen SOLE y no entienden mucho qué es lo que está pasando, cómo van a encontrar respuestas por Internet, cómo se maneja el computador, qué es lo que toca hacer en cada momento o quién es esa persona que intenta hablar con ellos desde quién sabe dónde.

Desde la experiencia, os digo: **el poder del Internet es como el poder del agua**… ¡se filtra por cualquier parte! ¡Se filtra por todas partes! Y es cuando un mensaje, como sea, desde el otro lado del mundo te dice “**estoy contigo,** esa pregunta que están haciendo está buenísima, yo estoy buscando y encontré (…) ¡felicitaciones por esa investigación, los espero para el siguiente SOLE!” y es ahí cuando **el ambiente se llena el ambiente de júbilo y nos hace saber a todos que aquí vinimos a aprender, que estamos JUNTOS. Y esos JUNTOS toman un sentido mucho más grande y más potente.**
