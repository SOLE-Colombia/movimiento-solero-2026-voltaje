## Welcome

Hi, I'm Sanjay, and I'm not an expert on connectivity, but I've learned by tinkering, trial and error, and researching online with others.

## What is managing a Jangala Big Box?

In this solution, you will be able to contact a charity in England that offers a very powerful portable modem that allows up to 100 people to connect to the internet at once, called the Big Box.

The organization that offers it is called Jangala and is dedicated to providing internet access to people in need of urgent humanitarian aid or longer-term development assistance.

**NOTE! Jangala defines criteria for who can receive and benefit from its equipment or support**.

It is important for you to know that these are not for personal use and that you must meet certain eligibility criteria. Based on the information you provide in the application form, they will assess whether your community or project can access their product.

We encourage you, together with your community, to invent a way in which internet connection could improve their living conditions and how they can use it for the common good. Develop your idea and tell Jangala about it. We are sure they will consider it.

## What's it for?

Imagine a community without connectivity. Without the technical knowledge to install, configure, and maintain a wireless network. Now imagine that, from one moment to the next, everyone can connect to a single Wi-Fi network.

What would happen? Among many things, an entire community could pay for a single internet plan and everyone could connect. Even better, they could connect from shared devices, such as computers or tablets, and enjoy faster speeds, a fun, and collaborative learning experience. That's what they could achieve if they managed this device: the [*Big Box*](https://www.janga.la/big-box) or [Jangala](http://janga.la/).

**Jangala's Big Box is a lightweight device, the size of a briefcase, that transforms any form of internet connectivity into Wi-Fi** that's easy to manage and scale, from tens to thousands of users. It's as if someone had a booster for a phone's internet connection, working for hundreds, or even thousands, of computers.

Internet access is desperately needed in many situations around the world: from people trying to establish contact after an earthquake to educational projects in remote areas. However, in these situations, the Internet is often unavailable: networks frequently go down in emergency situations, and deploying effective Internet access in remote areas is complicated, slow, and expensive.

**Who is Jangala?** Jangala is an NGO in the United Kingdom that seeks to solve this problem with its devices.

This solution will help you learn how to manage this powerful modem with the Jangala organization and receive support to adapt the Big Box to the needs of your context or, if you are part of an organization, to the conditions of the communities they work with.

If you are from a low-income community or in remote areas, you may be considered to receive one of their devices free of charge and for life.

## **What do you need?**

- An internet connection and a computer (you can also use a cell phone)
- Understanding English or translating with the online translator [***DeepL***](https://sole-sanjay.tiddlyhost.com/#www.deepl.com)
- Confidence in your management skills
- Digital exclusion conditions. Jangala will estimate the likelihood of receiving a Big Box
- Enthusiasm!

## How to do it?

- **Moment 1: Go to www.janga.la and look for the Big Box request form**
    
    Go to [www.janga.la](https://www.janga.la/request-a-box) to the "Request a Box" section. There you can decide if you need a **Get Box**, which is smaller, or a **Big Box**.
    The next steps concern requesting a Big Box
    
    ![](/https:/archive.org/download/f-71-b-658-b-290-e-48-d-3-be-45-e-4733-fe-4874-d/JangalaBixBox/JangalaBigBox_Home.png)
    
- **Moment 2: Complete the online form**
    
    In the Big Box section, you will find a button that says *Apply for a Big Box*.
    
    You must answer the questions on the form; this will help Jangala configure a Big Box that best meets your community's needs.
    
    The questionnaire is divided into four sections:
    
    - Section 1 asks questions about you and/or your organization.
    - Section 2 addresses the location for which you need a Big Box, including location, existing connectivity, and power supply.
    - Section 3 addresses Wi-Fi setup.
    - Section 4 contains final questions about how the Big Box will make a difference.
    
    If anything on this form is unclear or doesn't apply to your community's situation, please feel free to email [info@janga.la](mailto:info@janga.la).
    
    Filling out this form may take some time, and you may not have all the answers clear. Please try to answer as many questions as possible and **don't forget to submit**.
    
- **Moment 3: Wait for a response and establish a relationship with Jangala.**
    
    It may take a couple of weeks to receive a response via email from the Jangala team. They will contact you using the email address you registered on the form.
    
    Please be patient and listen for a response… this will be a very exciting moment!
    
    There may be additional questions or a virtual meeting with you may be required. During the meeting, you can clarify any additional concerns, discuss the specifics of your Big Box, and the process for receiving it by mail.
    
    The Jangala team is exceptional: very friendly and genuinely willing to help.
    
- **Moment 4: Plan and receive the Big Box by mail**
    
    After clarifying how Jangala will ship the device, it won't take more than a couple of weeks to receive the Big Box with the features you need.
    
    It's very important to ask the Jangala team to clearly state on the certified mail delivery that it's a **goods with no commercial value**. Depending on the country you're in, customs may require specific information about the device in order to receive the package.
    
    For example, in Colombia, it's necessary to include the IMEI number, which is used to register a cell phone, on the shipping invoice for a cell phone if it's included in the package you're sending. If this isn't done, the package must be nationalized at customs, which is a complex and expensive process. Either way, the Jangala team will be of great support in resolving any challenges, and they will cover those costs.
    
- **Moment 5: Install the Big Box with the support of the Jangala team**
    
    Finally, all that's left is to install the Big Box in the location where it will be used, and that's it!
    
    The Jangala team will offer support to guide you through plugging and using it. For example, you can do so through [***WhatsApp***](https://sole-sanjay.tiddlyhost.com/#WhatsApp) to offer almost immediate support. Once installed, you'll have Wi-Fi for 100 people within a 100-meter radius!
    
    Now all that's left is to use it for the common good.
    

## **How do I know if this solution works?**

Each Box provides Wi-Fi over any form of internet signal (3G/4G, landline, satellite), can use power from a variety of sources (mains, battery, cigarette lighter, renewable source, generator), and can use an external antenna to pick up 3G/4G signals where phones can't. You'll find more features below.

If you're a charity or group in need of connectivity, Jangala would love to hear from you. For organizations with limited financial means providing vital basic services to those affected by disasters or those in need of long-term development assistance, Jangala can provide systems as long-term, cost-free loans.

- How many people does it connect? 100 alone. With additional equipment, 1,000. With extensive equipment, up to 5,000.
- Can it be used in a mobile environment? Yes.
- Can it be used outdoors? Yes. It's waterproof and durable.
- Can it power additional Wi-Fi hotspots? Yes. They can be used to connect up to 5,000 people over a wide area.
- How many networks can it broadcast? Four. Each can be customized for content control, speed restrictions, and data restrictions.

## **Why might this solution work?**

The Jangala Big Box won't solve connectivity issues if there's no internet signal (wired, mobile, or satellite) in your area. But it does offer an opportunity to provide free or very low-cost connectivity for an entire community. For example, a school or a refugee camp. Imagine the learning opportunities if they use the internet together!
