# **Explore your cell phone WiFi connection passwords**

## Welcome

Hi, I'm Belen. I'm not an expert in technology and I've learned to use it as it has appeared. I don't deny it, I like to read the instructions of the devices I buy and I make it a point to understand many of the updates that are installed on my cell phone... lots of nerda!

## What's it like to know your cell phone WiFi passwords?

I remember when you had to put in your modem password every time you connected to the Internet. If there's one thing I appreciate about this new era of smartphones, it's the time savings of not having to type in the WiFi password every time you connect. But where do you store them, and how do you share those keys or passwords with anyone? Read on.

## What is it for?

With this solution you will be able to explore the **WiFi** passwords you already have stored in your cell phone. If you want to do SOLE in a place where you have been connected before, you can reconnect to the **WiFi** without having to re-enter all the characters of the password (which is sometimes a long string).

## What do you need?

- Cell phone with Android operating system.
- Internet connection.
- At least one [**WiFi**](https://sole-doc.tiddlyhost.com/#WiFi) password stored in your cell phone.
- Curiosity.

## How to do it?

- **Moment 1: Explore your passwords**
    
    First of all, access your phone's "Settings". Then, follow the "[**WiFi**](https://sole-doc.tiddlyhost.com/#WiFi)" section, where you will see all the available networks around you. Press the Internet network to which you already have access and for which you want to know the password. When you do so, a QR code will appear with this information. Then take a screenshot of this image.
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/Antena 3 4G 3 4G Antenna 22b2bd68c5b68076a24ccc4e1d6ff4af/image-800w.webp)
    
- **Step 2: Cracking the password**
    
    The next step is to be able to use a reader that has permission to be able to read this information. We cannot use the QR reader of our own phone, so we can use one online at the following page: [[https://zxing.org](https://zxing.org/](https://zxing.org](https://zxing.org/))
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/Conexión cable Ethernet Ethernet cable connection 22b2bd68c5b680fa90ceecc23bea76db/image 1-275w.webp)
    
    Once we are on this page, we upload our image and click on the "Submit Query" button. When we do this, the network name and password will appear so we can share it.
    
    ![Imagen](/assets/images/A pocket Wikipedia in your RaspberryPi 1a42bd68c5b680289571fc002238a69a/image 2-800w.webp)
    
- **Moment 3: You can now share it!**
    
    Now, all that remains is to share this information with the people who need it. Who wants to make a SOLE? Now that you can share the password, all the teams can start their research and solve the Big Question they want!
    

## How do you know if this solution works?

With this solution you will get to scan all the **WiFi** passwords you already have stored on your cell phone.

## Why would this solution work?

You have the possibility to know the saved passwords, but not those you haven't saved yet. To save them initially you have to ask the owner of the **WiFi** to share his password with you, but you will not have to ask for it again.
