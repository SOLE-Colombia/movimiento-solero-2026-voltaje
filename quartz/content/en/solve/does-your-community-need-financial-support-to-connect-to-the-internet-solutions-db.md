# Some organizations that support low-cost Internet

## Welcome

Hi, I'm Belen. Although I didn't write the original of this solution, I'm revising it today so you can understand it better. Sometimes we feel like we are alone, working against the tide. I would like you to know that it doesn't have to be that way, that there are people and organizations that can help you. I hope this solution will show you possible ways forward.

## What is it like to seek financial support in your community to connect to the Internet?

There are initiatives that seek to connect people who do not have access to the Internet, especially in rural, remote and low-income areas. 

They offer funding programs and resources for local communities and organizations that are working to build and expand Internet infrastructure, such as community networks and complementary access solutions. 

They also provide information on how to obtain low-cost Internet through government programs and non-profit organizations.

## **What are they good for?**

Contacting some of them can lead you to get internet access for your community, at affordable prices and with guarantees of maintenance, training and sustainability that you can't imagine right now... take the plunge!

## **What do you need?**

- A computer with a web browser and Internet connection.
- A web translator like [google translate](https://translate.google.com/?hl=es), [deepl](https://www.deepl.com/es/translator), or an artificial intelligence to help you translate (you can type in the search engine "ia to translate texts")
- Patience and time to dedicate to the search, better if you do it with others.
- A dose of courage to submit your application to one of the initiatives... you have all our encouragement! We did it this way.

    - https://mintic.gov.co/portal/715/w3-article-401110.html - "Connecting the unconnected" program by MINTIC and Colnodo
        
        The "Connecting the unconnected" program is an initiative of the Colombian Ministry of Information and Communication Technologies (MinTIC), which seeks to bring Internet to rural areas and communities with little or no connectivity. 
        
        The Digital Centers are free Wi-Fi access points installed in strategic locations to facilitate Internet access in these areas.
        
        **Steps to access, according to the program's website:**
        
        1. **Locate a Digital Center:** Digital Centers are located in rural areas and communities with poor connectivity. You can find information about their location on the [**MinTIC**](https://micrositios.mintic.gov.co/centros_digitales/) website.
        2. **Activate your device's Wi-Fi:** Turn on the Wi-Fi on your phone, tablet or other device you wish to connect.
        3. **Search for the "MinTIC Conecta" network:** Your device will display the available Wi-Fi networks. Select the network named "Mintic Conecta".
        4. **Fill in the requested data:** You will be prompted to enter certain information to register. Complete the required fields.
        5. **Accept the terms and conditions:** Carefully read the terms and conditions and, if you agree, accept them in order to connect.**Done!** Once you have completed the above steps, you will be able to use the Internet connection.
    - https://www.colnodo.apc.org/es/NOTAS/banda-de-900-mhz-para-las-comunidades-una-oportunidad-para-transformar-el-acceso-a-internet-en-zonas-rurales
        
        In Colombia, access to the 900 MHz band for communities is being addressed **through a public consultation led by the Ministry of Information and Communication Technologies (MinTIC) and the National Spectrum Agency (ANE).** 
        
        The objective is to make the use of this band more flexible for community networks, especially in rural areas with little or no commercial network coverage.
        
        **Process and Participation:**
        
        - **Public Consultation:** A public consultation has been opened so that organized communities, community networks and other interested stakeholders can express their interest and provide comments on the proposal.
        - **Participation Form:** An online form is available for communities to describe their situation, interests and suggestions on the use of the band.
        - **Information and Documentation:** The ANE has published documents and guidelines for the consultation, which are available for viewing and downloading.
        - **Deadline:** The public consultation is open until July 1, 2025.
        
        **Focus on Rural Areas:**
        
        - The 900 MHz band, with its capacity for good coverage and penetration, is considered ideal for supporting connectivity in rural and remote areas.
        - It seeks to enable community networks to take advantage of this band to bring connectivity to places where there is no or limited commercial coverage.
        
        **Potential Benefits:**
        
        - **Community Connectivity:** Facilitate internet access for rural communities through community networks.
        - **Economic and Social Development:** Boosting access to services such as telemedicine, virtual education and other opportunities through connectivity.
        - **Community Empowerment:** Community networks can generate experience and autonomy in managing their own networks.
        - **Future Readiness:** Experience with this band can pave the way for the adoption of more advanced technologies and services in the future.
        
        **Active Participation:**
        
        It is crucial that interested communities actively participate in the publicconsultation, expressing their needs, expectations and proposals for the use of the 900 MHz band. The participation of all stakeholders is essential to build a strategy that ensures fair and sustainable connectivity for all.
        
        In summary, the 900 MHz band represents an opportunity for Colombian communities to access the Internet and improve their connectivity, especially in rural areas, through community networks. 
        
        The public consultation is the space for communities to express their needs and contribute to the construction of a public policy that recognizes the importance of the spectrum for social and economic development.

        - Provides training and resources for community members to develop skills in building, managing and maintaining community networks.
        - If they already have a network, NUPEF can help strengthen it by improving infrastructure, upgrading technology and providing technical support to ensure its continued operation.
        - Promotes the creation of safe and welcoming online communities, where diversity is valued and users are protected from violence and abuse.

## **How do you know if this solution works?**

How it can help to have low cost internet:

- **Infrastructure funding:** there are organizations that offer funding to organizations that build and expand internet networks in underserved areas, which can result in more affordable options for residents.
- **Information on subsidy programs:** other organizations provide information on government and non-government programs that offer subsidies or reduced rates for Internet access for low-income people.
- **Community networks:** Others support the creation of community networks, which are often a cheaper alternative to commercial options.
- **Resources for building networks:** some others offer a toolkit for building and maintaining community networks, which can help reduce infrastructure costs.
- **Training** for installation and maintenance of equipment and networks.
- **Strengthening the social fabric and links** between the community and with the community of practice: usually initiatives include content and practice in digital rights, equity, community ties and human-to-human connection, which make the internet an excuse for more aware and connected communities.
