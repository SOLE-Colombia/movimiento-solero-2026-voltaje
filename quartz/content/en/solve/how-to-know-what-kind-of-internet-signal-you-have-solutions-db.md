# Find out how the internet reaches your device

## Welcome

Hi, I'm Belen. I'm not an internet expert, in fact, I feel old and I don't understand a lot of things about connectivity. If you also feel like me, this information will enlighten you. If you don't feel like me, but you want to better understand the differences between signals and how they relate to the operation of the devices, this information will get you out of doubt. 

## What is it to know the type of signal you have on your device?

Internet signals can be classified in several ways, in SOLE Colombia **we have chosen this classification, according to the means by which the signals are transmitted**. 

At the moment, the signals that exist are: 

- **Cable signal, the fastest and most stable**
    - **Fiber optic**: it uses pulsed light to transmit data through special cables. It is the fastest and most stable type of signal known.
    - **Coaxial cable:** transmits data through copper cables (also used for cable TV).
    - **DSL** (Digital Subscriber Line): uses telephone lines to transmit data. It is less fast, but uses a network that has existed for a long time, so it already reaches many places and does not require a new installation.
- **Wireless signal, which connects over the air and requires signal repeaters.**
    - **Wi-Fi** (Wireless Fidelity): uses radio waves, in different standards, to broadcast the signal to the devices that make up your network.
    - **Mobile network** (3G/4G/5G): they use the infrastructure of mobile telephony signals to provide Internet access and move in a specific wave spectrum. The difference between them has to do with speed, 3G is the slowest and oldest, 5G the fastest and most recent. Some devices available on the market were not built to support 5G and it is not available in many parts of the world because the developments needed to detect the wave spectrum are not yet developed. As a tidbit, I tell you that 5G technology still generates mistrust regarding data security and the lack of spectrum regulation limits the developments needed to incorporate it worldwide 🫢
- **Satellite, which travels through space and requires a satellite dish.**
    - **GEO, or based on geostationary orbit satellites**: they are far away from the Earth and provide greater stability, which makes them ideal for remote areas. You may be familiar with names like Viasat and HugesNet, which are providers of this technology.
    - **LEO, or low earth orbit satellite-based**: they are closer to the Earth, between 500 and 2000 km. They are faster compared to GEO, but require more devices to cover the same area. The names of the providers of this service may ring a bell: Starlink, OneWeb, Amazon Kuiper, and Telesat Lightspeed.
    - **MEO, or medium orbit satellite-based**: these are located between LEO and GEO and are the least common. The marketing of this type of connection has been aimed at sectors such as navigation (GPS) and high-end business services, so they are not as visible to the average end user.

Depending on the type of device you have, you will have access to different possible sources of Internet signal. Below you can see what type of signal each type of device allows:

Devices and signals:

| **Device** | **Wired** | **Wifi** | **Mobile** | **Satellite** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Computer** | Some | All | None | None |
| **Tablet** | None | All | Some | None |
| **Cellular** | None | All | All | None |
| **Smart TV** | Some | All | None | None |
| **IOT (Internet of Things)** | Some | All | None | None |

## How to do it?

### How do you know if you have a cable signal?

Having a cable internet service guarantees that you are getting 100% of the signal that your provider gives you. If you have an ethernet cable connected to your computer, you should have a wired signal.

- **What to do if your connection is not working?**
    1. **Check the lights on the router**: look for the ones that indicate the **Ethernet connection** (they are usually green/orange and blink when there is activity). If they are red or not lit, check the mains connection. You can reboot the router and start over, it almost always works.
    2. **Check the connection on the device**: check the Ethernet connection status in your computer's settings, it should confirm that you are connected.
    3. **Check the Ethernet cable:** check if it is properly connected, on the router and on your device, and that it is in good condition. 
    4. If nothing works, contact your service provider.

### How do you know if you have Wi-Fi signal?

Looking at the WiFi icon 🛜 is the quickest way to check your connection: the more bars that are colored, the better your signal. If the icon has a warning sign, it means there is a problem or it is not available. If the icon is disabled or has a line through it, it means it is turned off and you should turn it on.

- **What to do if your connection is not working?**
    1. **Access the Wi-Fi settings:** from your device's settings, you can access the networks that are available. If one of them appears as "connected", you are connected to that network. If you do not see "connected" to any of them, you may need to choose one and access it with a password, or the signal may be weak. You can try turning the router off and on again, and also the computer, it often works to reset all the information and start from scratch. It is also useful to put the device in 'airplane mode'.
    2. **Check the signal strength:** in the device's settings you can check how strong the signal is for each available network. Moving closer to the router or removing obstacles between you can improve the signal. 
    3. If nothing works, contact your service provider, there may be an error in the configuration.

### How do you know if you have a mobile signal?

Look in a top corner of your cell phone. There you will find some mobile signal bars 📶: if you see one or more bars, the phone is receiving signal and reading your SIM card. You can also confirm this in your phone's settings and check the signal strength. 

- **What to do if your connection is not working?**
    1. **Check that your SIM card is properly installed:** it is through the card that your mobile connection provider authorizes you to use their network. It has happened to me that I have put the SIM card upside down in my mobile device and there was no way to connect or call.
    2. **Activate mobile data: make**sure that 'mobile data' is active, as well as 'mobile networks'.
    3. **Check the signal strength:** usually this information is under 'mobile networks' and 'data usage', or under 'about phone' and 'signal strength' and can be found on the device settings icon. 
    4. **Check the network settings:** you can check if there are any errors in the network settings, if you don't understand much, you can call your service provider. 
    5. **Check your** signal provider'**s coverage maps**: you may be in an area where there is no coverage and you will have to configure one of the other Internet connection options. 
    6. If nothing works, contact your service provider, there may be an error in the configuration.

### How do you know if you have satellite signal?

Installing the satellite signal implies that you have had to install a software package to be able to operate the connection with your satellite dish **📡** If your dish is properly installed and pointing towards the satellite, the weather is good and you have no clouds or storm on the way, the router turned on and a good visibility, you should be able to enjoy good connection.

- **What to do if the connection does not work?**
    1. **Check the physical conditions of the satellite dish**: it must be installed correctly, in a place with a clear view of the sky, with no obstacles such as buildings or trees around it. 
    2. **Check the weather conditions:** the presence of clouds or approaching thunderstorms can affect the satellite signal.
    3. **Check the orientation of the antenna towards the satellite:** for this, you will need to check the elevation (vertical) and azimuth (horizontal) angles. For this you will need a compass and an antenna alignment application, as well as a signal meter. You can make progressive adjustments to fine-tune the signal reception.
    4. **Verify the router connection:** check that it is turned on, the Ethernet cable is correctly connected to the satellite modem and the router and that the cable is in good condition.
    5. **Check the signal on your device:** some satellite internet systems have signal monitoring software that can provide you with valuable data to know if it is connected correctly.
    6. **Test the speed:** you can do a**speed** check by consulting [an Internet scientist](/ser-un-cientifico-del-internet-voltaje-desconectado)**.** 
    7. If nothing works, contact your service provider, there may be an error in the configuration.
