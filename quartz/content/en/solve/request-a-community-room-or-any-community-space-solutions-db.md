# Public spaces are also yours

## Welcome

Hi, I'm Manu. As a SOLE Explorer I have had to learn how to ask for community spaces in the places where we have gone to do SOLE and I know that it is not always easy to know where to start. 

## What is it like to ask for a community room, or any community space?

This is a solution that guides you on how you can reserve a communal space to use the Internet as a group, or for whatever it is you want to do in this space. Remember that each space has its own rules and application procedures, so this solution can guide you on the criteria, but it is not a definitive solution.

If you have any suggestions on how you do it in your community, that you think may help other people, I invite you to comment on the solution, the Internet is created by all of us!

## What is it for?

In SOLE Colombia we believe that it is more fun when we do things together. Communal spaces are spaces for us to meet with others, to connect, to celebrate, to share, and you can use them too. 

Here are some steps you can follow to reserve the communal room closest to you.

Good luck!

## **What do you need?**

- Good attitude, believe in yourself
- Identity document to sign the reservation (OPTIONAL)
- Letter to request the reservation of the space (OPTIONAL)

## **How to do it?**

- **Moment 1: Discover the place and go to it.**
    
    Once you have decided where you want to hold your activity, go to the community hall and talk to the people in charge. 
    
    Introduce yourself to them and tell them why you want to use the space, what you want to use it for, and how many people have confirmed. 
    
    Also encourage them to participate in your activity, for example, if you are going to do a SOLE, you can invite them to become participants.

- **Moment 2: Ask about the requirements, what you need to formalize the application.**
    
    <img src="/assets\images\Conexión cable Ethernet Ethernet cable connection 22b2bd68c5b680fa90ceecc23bea76db\image 1-275w.webp" alt="image.png" srcset="/assets\images\Conexión cable Ethernet Ethernet cable connection 22b2bd68c5b680fa90ceecc23bea76db\image 1-275w.webp 275w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 400px, (max-width: 1200px) 800px, 1200px" loading="lazy" />
    
    Once you are in contact with the people in charge, ask them about the availability of the place and what requirements you need, or how you can book it.
    
- **Step 3: Make the reservation letter or meet the requirements of the venue**
    
    Usually, community halls require a letter to reserve the venue. 
    
    Write down what the activity will consist of, when it will take place and who the organizers and participants will be. 
    
    It is also very important that you include your contact details, they will need them to confirm if it is possible for your activity to take place at the time you have planned. 
    
    We also advise you to include a few words of thanks for the authorization to use the space. 
    
    You may need to attach a photocopy of your identity document, so be prepared for this.
    
    Do you think an example could help you? Here are the steps we have to take into account and how the example would look like:
    
    1. Include place and date where you are.
    2. Head the letter with the name or denomination of the person or entity that owns or manages the space.
    3. Introduce yourself as the person responsible for the application or organization of the activity.
    4. Explain the purpose of the activity and the details of the activity. You can also request elements that you need to carry out your activity, for example, chairs, tables, computers or other elements that you require and that are within the reach of whoever is lending you the room.
    5. Thank them for their attention and let them know how they can give you an answer. It may be your phone number or a way to contact you. 
    6. Finally, sign the letter. 
    
    This is what this written form would look like:
    
    
💡 Vereda El Montecillo Municipality of Arracachal
    
    February 13, 2038
    
    Good morning,
    
    My name is (your full name), I am identified with CC No. (Cédula Number), and I am contacting you to request the use of the (name of the space or meeting room) to carry out an activity, in the company of people from the community.
    
    My idea is to use the space to learn, dialogue and solve some challenges of my community that we will identify in the meeting, using the Internet as a group.
    
    The details of the meeting are: - Date: on (day of the week), (day) of (month) of (year) - Time: at (time) - Number of people: we will meet (number) people - Duration of the meeting: (time)
    
    To carry out the activity, we need some conditions and furniture: one chair per person, some tables where we can place some computers and the possibility to plug them in (so they don't run out of battery). It is also necessary to have electricity and Internet connection available. 
    
    We commit ourselves to take care of the common space and to comply with the rules of use in this space of all.
    
    You can contact me at (phone number) or through (other means of contact).
    
    Thank you very much for your attention, I look forward to hearing from you
    
    (Name of the person who signs).

    *Remember that this is only an example, you can elaborate the letter as you wish. Don't forget to ask the person in charge what information, data or attachments you should include in the document.*
    
- **Step 4: Request a follow-up contact and ask for confirmation, don't wait too long.**
    
    It may happen that your confirmation needs to pass some filters or that the process takes some time. Although you should not lose your composure, it is a good idea to ask for the contact person's information to follow up on the process, so you can make sure that you can plan and carry out your activity within the deadlines you had planned.
    
- **Moment 5: Thank the management and celebrate, you have found an ally in your community!**
    
    Once you have received confirmation that you can use the space, don't forget to thank the person in charge with whom you had contact from the beginning. 
    
    You can write her an email, a WhatsApp message or greet her in the street reminding her how important her support was to get the activity carried out. It's a good ally for future occasions when you want to use the community room again.
    

Let us know how you did booking the community hall in your sidewalk, community or complex! :)

## How do you know if this solution works?

Once you have your space reserved, it's just a matter of iterating the process as many times as you need to. You may be able to make a larger request that lasts longer. To do this, it may be useful to draw on the good news, findings and successes of the activity you have proposed in the space and how it is bearing fruit in the community. 

For example, when you get a group of people to do SOLE on a recurring basis, you can see the benefits it has for skills such as communication, negotiation, reading comprehension, digital skills, autonomy, leadership... making a small report, for example, with the result of the Reflection capsules (available on the SOLE Colombia website https://n9.cl/reflexionatusole, can be very powerful to strengthen the relationship with your ally and with the people in your community.

## Why might this solution work?

Sometimes, procedures involving large entities, or involving the decision of many people, can take time. It is important that, at the time of the application, you are clear about the deadlines and can set reminders to follow up on your request. 

Often it is enough to remember the person with whom you made the request, stop by to say hello politely and remind them that you are waiting for a response. He/she will thank you for your attention and will surely put more love in order to have an answer to give you.
