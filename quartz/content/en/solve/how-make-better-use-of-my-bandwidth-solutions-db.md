# **Make better use** of your Internet service options

## Welcome

Hi, I'm Manu. I'm not an expert in technology, but I know how frustrating it is when the movie freezes or the video call cuts off. So, after doing some research, I found some tricks to improve the connection at home that I want to share with you. 

## What is leveraging bandwidth?

Leveraging the options of the internet connection service you have is related to using all the settings and tools available to optimize your online experience, in terms of performance, speed, security and data efficiency. 

## What is it for?

Did you know that in the Internet world there are two terms that are often confused? I'm talking about **bandwidth** and **speed**. Let's break them down and I will give you some tips to get the most out of your bandwidth and speed, contracted in your home Internet service. 

### **Bandwidth and Speed: What's that?**

Imagine that **bandwidth** is like a giant truckload of goods, so it can carry a lot of packets (data) at once. The bigger the truck, the more packets it can carry. But here comes the key: **speed** is the time it takes, for that truck, to reach its destination. So, even if you have a huge truck (high bandwidth), if it doesn't go fast, the packets will be waiting!

**In conclusion:** A high bandwidth allows you to move a lot of data at the same time, but, if the speed is slow, that data will take a long time to arrive. And that's what can frustrate us when we are trying to watch a video or make a video call!

## What do you need?

- Wi-Fi Internet service at home
- Patience and curiosity

## **How to do it?**

- **Moment 1: Use the network cable**
    
    If you can, connect your computer to your router with a network cable. This is like going from a truck to a high-speed train - the difference is impressive! The wired connection will give you maximum speed and stability.

- **Moment 2: Connect to 5GHz if You Use Wi-Fi**

    If you can't use cable, the next best option is to connect to the 5GHz band of your router. This is like having access to a faster lane on a highway: less traffic, more speed. Just keep in mind that it works best when you're close to the router.
    
- **Moment 3: Don't Forget 2.4GHz**
    
    
    If you're a bit far from the router, and you see that the connection gets slow, switch to the 2.4GHz band. Although it's a bit slower than 5GHz, it can be more reliable. Think of it as a rural road that's always available, even if it's not as fast.

- **Moment 4: Adjust the Wi-Fi Channel**
    
    Sometimes, the connection feels slow because there are too many people in your neighborhood using the same Wi-Fi channel. Changing the channel can make your connection faster and smoother. It's like moving your party to a place where there is less noise.

- **Moment 5: Keep Your Router in Shape**
    
    
    Finally, make sure your router is well placed and up to date. Place it in a ventilated place and away from other interfering devices. A well-maintained router is key to have a good connection.

## How to know if this solution works?

If you have checked your speed, network configuration and data consumption on your devices and they are consistent with the data in your contract with your service provider, you can be sure that you are getting the most out of your Internet service.

By following these steps, you will not only have a better understanding of your bandwidth and speed, but you will also be able to optimize your connection and enjoy your online activities without any problems.

## Why would this solution work?

To ensure that you are getting the most out of your service, in addition to checking speed and bandwidth, you can test at different times of the day, sometimes speeds may vary according to general network saturation. 

You can also monitor your data consumption, the data usage of the applications and programs you use and set some limits to make your consumption more consistent.
