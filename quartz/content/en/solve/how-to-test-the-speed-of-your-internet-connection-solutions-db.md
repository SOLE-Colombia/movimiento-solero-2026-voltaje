## What is an internet connection speed test?

An internet connection speed test, or speed test, **is a tool that measures the speed of your internet connection, evaluating the download and upload speed, as well as the latency ([ping](/latencia-latency-conceptorio))**. 

## **What is it useful for?**

These tests are useful to verify if the speed contracted with your provider matches the actual speed you receive. It also helps you identify connection problems and make informed decisions about your Internet service provider.

## **What do you need?**

- The device with which you are going to connect to the Internet.
- Access a website, or download a measurement app, such as [**Speedtest.net**](https://www.speedtest.net/es), [**Fast.com**](https://fast.com/es/), or nPerf.com. There are also mobile applications such as [**Speedtest by Ookla**](https://www.speedtest.net/es/mobile/android/help).

## **How to do it?**

- **Step 1: Access to a speed test service**
    
    You can use websites such as [**Speedtest.net**](https://www.speedtest.net/es), [**Fast.com**](https://fast.com/es/), or nPerf.com. There are also mobile applications such as [**Speedtest by Ookla**](https://www.speedtest.net/es/mobile/android/help).

    Start the test on the website or app.

- **Step 3: Interpret the results**
    
    The results generally show the download speed, upload speed and [latency](/latencia-latency-conceptorio) (ping). 
    
    - **Download speed:** This is the speed at which you can receive data from the Internet, such as downloading files or watching videos.
    - **Upload speed:** The speed at which you can send data to the Internet, such as sending emails or uploading videos to social networks.
    - **Latency (ping):** The time it takes for a signal to go back and forth between your device and a remote server, measured in milliseconds. A low ping is preferable, especially for online gaming or video calls.

**Additional considerations:**

- [**Wired](/conexion-cable-ethernet-ethernet-cable-connection-conceptorio) vs. [WiFi](/wifi-fidelidad-inalambrica-wifi-wireless-fidelity-conceptorio)[connection](/conexion-cable-ethernet-ethernet-cable-connection-conceptorio):** Wired (Ethernet) speed tests are usually more accurate than WiFi tests, as WiFi connection can be affected by interference.
- [**Test servers](/servidor-server-conceptorio):** Some services allow you to choose the test server for more accurate results or to test different locations.
- **Interpret the results:** Compare the results with the speed contracted with your provider and consider factors such as time of day and activity on your network, as this may affect the results.

## **How do you know if this solution works?**

- **Verify your connection performance:** Allows you to confirm if you are getting the speed contracted with your Internet provider.
- **Diagnose connection problems:** If the results are low, it may be an indication of problems with your provider or with your network configuration.
- **Optimize your online experience:** By knowing your connection speed, you can adjust video quality, avoid downloading large files during peak hours, or even consider switching providers if necessary.
- **Help you make decisions:** If you are considering switching internet plans, the speed test helps you choose the most suitable plan for your needs.
