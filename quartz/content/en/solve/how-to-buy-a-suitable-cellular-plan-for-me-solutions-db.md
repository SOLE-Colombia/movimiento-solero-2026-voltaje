## **Before we start, some ethical issues**

At SOLE Voltage, we want to clarify the ethical implication of the decisions we make, and we like to put the focus on the circumstance that leads you to make that decision. We often use uncomfortable questions to challenge ourselves.

## How to do it?

- **Before we start: ALERT! prepaid or postpaid?**
    
    In order to have clarity when reading this solution, first, we must address some concepts. In this case, the difference between a **postpaid and a prepaid plan.** 
    
    | **Prepaid** | **Postpaid** |
    | --- | --- |
    | It is when the user pays for mobile services before using them. | This is when the user pays for mobile services after using them. |
    | You can recharge whenever you want. | Month to month you will have to pay your bill. |
    | You can purchase your prepaid plan by subscription or on demand. | Promotes your credit life. |
    | You don't get a monthly bill. | Gives you additional benefits such as lower rates |
    
    There is no absolute answer on the type of service to choose. It depends on the habits, possibilities and needs of each person.
    
- **Moment 0: How do I know if I need to buy a cell phone plan?**
    
    One of the characteristics of our current times is the great development of international and national markets. Our cell phones are made in China and we can buy them in Colombia, we can go to the market and buy rice brought from Brazil. In addition, we find disproportionate consumption options, we can buy white rice, yellow rice, bulk rice, lemon-flavored rice, etc. 
    
    There are endless options to turn us into consumers of products that, many times, we do not need. Thus, we end up buying non-nutritious foods and fall back on expenses that do not satisfy the main function of food. 
    
    In the same way, we fill our homes with an endless amount of merchandise that ends up piled up or discarded because, in the end, they are of no use to us. 
    
    At SOLE Colombia, we want to promote **responsible consumption.** 
    
    Is the choice of your cell phone plan with data a responsible choice? We encourage you to answer the following questions:
    
    - Do you need to buy a cellular plan or do you have other options to access the internet?
    - Is there another option to supplement your Internet connection that can be paid for by several people?
    - If you buy a cellular plan, will you really improve your quality of life or will it get worse? how much time will you be connected to social networks? will it be difficult for you to disconnect from content? will it involve a dose of monthly stress to know how you will pay for this new expense?
    - Finally, is this new consumption really a priority for you? what will you actually use it for?
    - Don't forget this consideration: the decision you make may be reversible when conditions change. It's not a child, it's just a payment plan that you can change as and when you need to.
- **Moment 1: Why do I need a cellular plan?**
    
    Now that you've decided, it's time to understand what you need it for. 
    
    Do you need the plan to facilitate your work, to study, to stay connected with your family and friends, to spend leisure time? Depending on your answer you will identify usage patterns. 
    
    Let's use my case as an example:
    
    
💡 In 2022 I had two jobs and no office. In addition, I was very mobile: I traveled around Bogota and to different parts of the country on a recurring basis. I needed to connect to virtual meetings, one of my jobs required Zoom video calls. So I needed a data connection and to make calls to many people. At that time, I decided to get a postpaid plan with 10 Gb and unlimited minutes. Soon after, I started to travel and stay constantly at a family farm, where the data signal was very bad. I decided to buy a fixed internet plan for the farm. In 2023 I only had one job and I had an office with internet. I no longer needed to travel as much. I changed my postpaid plan to a cheaper one, and cancelled the internet plan at the farm.

    
    Now it's your turn to identify what you need the internet plan for!
    
- **Moment 2: According to what you need, these are the questions to ask yourself**
    - Do you need minutes, data or both?
    - Do you need a high connection speed to download large files or have video calls?
    - Do you constantly need to have this service or is it only for a few days of the week?
    - Do you need coverage in multiple territories or just one?
    - What budget do you have available for this expense?
    
- **Momento 3: De acuerdo con lo que está disponible, estas son las preguntas para hacerte**
    
    ¡Ahora vamos a la acción! necesitamos conseguir la siguiente información:
    
    **¿Qué operadores móviles están disponibles en mi territorio?**
    
    - Pregúntale a tus vecinos ¿qué operador usan o han usado en el pasado? ¿cuál funciona mejor? ¿cuál tiene mejor asistencia técnica? ¿cuál tiene mejores tarifas?
    - Puedes ir a las tiendas de telefonía celular en el centro urbano más cercano y preguntar directamente sobre que operadores tienen disponibles. En Colombia para 2024 hay múltiples opciones: **Claro, Movistar, TIGO, WOM, Virgin Mobile, Avantel y Móvil Éxito** son los principales operadores del mercado. Estos dos últimos son operadores pequeños que alquilan las infraestructura de los grandes operadores para prestar sus servicios.
    
    **¿Qué planes tienen disponibles estos operadores?**

- **Moment 4: Evaluate which is the best option: What do I really need?**
    
    It's time to make a decision! With the information you gathered, we will give you some criteria that we consider fundamental when making a decision:
    
    - **Frequency of use, how often will you use it?**
        
        
        ![Pixabay License, [https://pixabay.com/photos/iphone-smartphone-apps-apple-inc-410324/](https://pixabay.com/photos/iphone-smartphone-apps-apple-inc-410324/)](%C2%BFC%C3%B3mo%20comprar%20un%20plan%20celular%20adecuado%20para%20mi%2012a2bd68c5b680b790a0ed2d55ea777b/pre_02.jpg)
        
        Pixabay License, [https://pixabay.com/photos/iphone-smartphone-apps-apple-inc-410324/](https://pixabay.com/photos/iphone-smartphone-apps-apple-inc-410324/)
        
        If, for example, **most of your days** you need to make calls and be connected to the Internet, on balance, postpaid plans end up being the best option. 
        
        But if, on the other hand, your **demand is occasional**, has variability throughout the month and/or you may be constantly connected to a [**WiFi**](https://www.notion.so/c8df36bd0025488fae657e72d045b5ef?pvs=21) network at your workplace or home, then sporadic prepaid recharges may be the most useful.
        
    - **Budget, how much money do you want to spend?**
        
        Now it's time to set a budget! An easy count: how many hours of work per week does this new consumption represent? This may become a permanent expense, so it is important to reason the purchase.
        
        **The final decision may come down to this point:** people who use prepaid plans usually do not have a constant flow of money to ensure they pay a monthly fee. While the use of postpaid is associated with people who can guarantee the payment of a monthly fee on a constant basis.
        
        Currently, prepaid recharges, in the Colombian market, start at **$2,000 COP with one day of validity.** On the other hand, the cheapest postpaid plans are around **$27,000 COP** per month**.** 
        
        To make this decision make a count of how much you are willing to invest in this service and what is your frequency of use.
        
    - **Usage habits, for what and how are you going to use it?**
        
        Are you a person who makes long calls or do you spend most of your time connected to the Internet? Depending on this, you can prioritize the **number of minutes, the number of Gigabytes** or both, when choosing your consumption option. 
        
        Below, we explain each of these variables if you are still not sure how they work:
        
        | **Minutes** | **Data** |
        | --- | --- |
        | The minutes associated to your plan determine **how much time you can spend talking to other cell phones nationwide**, in some cases it also includes a number of minutes to other countries such as Venezuela, Canada or the United States. **Currently, most postpaid plans come with unlimited minutes.** | These are the **amount of mobile data destined for internet use on your phone**. Its use is the amount that your cell phone uploads or downloads when you use it. You can configure them to not spend them, save them or restrict what they are used for. Remember that depending on the content you upload or download, the data consumption will be higher or lower, for example, **watching a two-hour movie from your cell phone can consume approximately 300 MB**, almost a third of a Gigabyte. Content such as videos, audios or high resolution images have a high data consumption compared to other types of files such as texts. |
        |  |  |

        For example, when we do SOLE, we need to connect a few computers in order to do research on Big Questions. 
        
        That means that, the plan we hire, must necessarily have data. 
        
        You can make your phone's Internet connection available to the group, so that everyone can use it. 
        
        Although Internet searches that take place on a SOLE do not use a lot of data, they may want to watch videos or other content that requires more data than you have contracted. However, you don't need that much data available all the time. 
        
        So you can buy a prepaid plan that allows them to stay connected, on time for the SOLE, and solve their Big Questions. 
        
        If you decide to see each other once a week or you want to have the space available with the connection at any time, you can consider paying for a postpaid plan between all of you? How does that sound?
        
    - **Signal, where are you going to use it?**
        
        We have to look at coverage, upload and download speed, 3G, 4G signal. 
        
        **Although we often hear people say that one operator is better than another, this will depend on the region and can change over time.** 
        
        Sometimes they install more antennas or their networks become saturated due to an excess of users.
        
    - **Other benefits, information that may be useful**
        
        **Beware of "Free data usage in applications".**
        
        **Remember, nothing is free!** Most plans currently in Colombia have the use of applications such as *Facebook or* *WhatsApp* in an "unlimited" way for the duration of the charge or for the entire month in case of postpaid plans. 
        
        They never explain to us that this unlimited consumption **is only related to messages containing texts**, that is, if you see a photo or a video, by Whatsapp or Facebook, the data associated with this consumption will be deducted from your plan.
        
        **Free' applications**
        
        Some postpaid plans offer us license of applications such as *Amazon Prime* (series and movies platform) or *Spotify* (music playback application), i.e. for the payment of a cellular plan they give you access to the Premium version of these platforms. This can be beneficial if they are platforms that you already use, if not, ignore it, it will be just another consumption.
        
        They also do not tell you that this way of 'forcing' you to use certain applications to communicate, entertain you, inform you or create your digital presence, is part of a commercial strategy. That is, companies will use the information from your profiles to offer you consumer goods, which will benefit the corporations responsible for their processing... in other words, these companies are profiting from the information they obtain about you. So, be careful, when a product is free, it is because you are the product.
        

## **Don't be fooled**

It is important that you constantly check the operators' plans and offers. 

Currently, in Colombia, you can do **"portability",** that is, switch from one operator to another, change from one operator's plan to another while keeping your number, just by requesting the new operator to do this process**. There are no longer any permanence clauses that oblige you to stay with the same operator for a certain period of time**. So if you see that there is a better option for you ****do not hesitate to port!

There is a small offer available in small operators, these are prepaid plans with long terms. 

I, who live in Bogota, am evaluating switching to Movil Exito. I currently have a postpaid plan with WOM where I **pay $40,000 COP**, this entitles me to **60 Gb and unlimited minutes**, that's a lot! Initially, I switched to WOM because, by doing portability, they gave me the **first three months free, plus 20Gb** and **they charged me $35,000 COP.** Now that I am in Bogota and work from home and office, I have internet available from these places. I was reviewing options while writing this solution and I saw that Móvil Éxito offers the following prepaid plans:

Per month, my consumption does not exceed 5 Gb and I make, by far, 30 calls per month. For this reason, the first plan would cost me **$13,000 COP, less than a third of what I pay now!** Yes, I have to pay for the whole year at once, but I am not tied to any kind of contract. 

## **How much is enough?**

This question is difficult to answer, but I think we can rephrase it as follows: **does the option you chose meet the need and intention you projected at the beginning?** 

In the previous example, I showed you how I consider that the plan I currently have is excessive and I found options that do meet my initial requirement, at a third of the price I currently pay. 

This type of reasoning is what we would like you to have when making a decision about the option you are going to buy.
