## What is it for?

There are places where the cell phone signal is very weak, you can see it in the connection bars of your cell phone. For example, you see a single bar or something that says *H+,* *E or* even that there is no signal. An antenna, which has the necessary characteristics for the conditions of the place from where you want to connect, can help you to get a better connection quality. 

I recently understood that the Internet signal that reaches cell phones travels through invisible electromagnetic waves, and that cell phones have a small antenna inside them that captures those waves, allowing us to have access to the Internet. 

What the antenna does, simply, is to capture and amplify the specific data signal of the cell phone (3G or 4G): the signal travels, in electromagnetic waves, through the air, from a higher place than the one you are in -above trees, buildings or hills- and goes down, through a cable, to your height. Once at that point, the signal can be shared by generating a WiFi network, or by cable, using a [modem or router](https://www.notion.so/1512bd68c5b680359894c66ee55ee27d?pvs=21). I know these terms may seem confusing, but as you go through the installation steps, I hope you will be able to understand them more clearly.

## Before we begin, a few safety issues

Remember that you are working with electrical equipment. Be careful when connecting electrical power. Be aware of weather conditions, especially the risk of thunderstorms: the antenna, when installed, would act as a lightning rod. A lightning strike on the antenna could cause serious personal and material damage.

💡 **How is internet usage measured?** Internet data usage is measured by the amount of data that is downloaded to your computer or cell phone when you make a query, for example, watch a video, listen to a song, read an article in an online newspaper or receive a text message. The "weight" of each of these files is measured in *bytes* of information and the most common measurement when you buy a data plan is Gigabytes, Gb or *Gigabytes.* To give you a rough reference, 1 Giga or 1 Gigabyte (which is 1 million *bytes*) is equivalent to 30 minutes of video in high definition (HD) or 2 hours in low definition (SD); or 200 songs; or 4 hours of video calls; or 600 web pages; or 350,000 text messages. As you may have noticed, a video is the most "heavy" and a text message is the least "heavy". That's why phone operators sometimes "give" you free text messages but charge you to watch videos.

        
        
💡 **How fast can you access information on the Internet?** As well as knowing how much they "weigh" it is important to know how fast you can access the files you are consulting. This is measured in *Megabytes per second or Mbps* that are downloaded to your computer or cell phone. Having more than 5 Mbps of download allows you to watch a video without being cut off and have 4 other computers doing internet queries at the same time. Having between 1 and 5 Mbps allows you to make a video call although it may have interruptions. Having less than 1 Mbps allows you to only consult web pages with patience or send and receive text messages. If you want to play video games or watch movies online you probably need at least 25 Mbps download.

## **What can you do?**

Among many connectivity solutions I have tried, this is one of the best to create a space to access the Internet together, allowing several people to connect to a wifi signal at the same time in places where the 3 or 4G signal is weak. If there is no cell phone coverage, this solution will not work. However, telephony coverage is gradually increasing all over the planet.

I recommend it not only for the home, but for common spaces such as schools, libraries, businesses or community halls. It can also be used in public spaces such as parks if they have a place to leave it under insurance and with constant electricity. 

---

## **How far can you go?**

It is not an economical solution, but I have seen that it can have a great reach as long as it becomes a community internet access point. To do this, it is necessary for the community to agree to buy it with contributions from the members and maintain it together. This may be a considerable initial expense, but it is more economical over time than if each person buys his or her own data plan individually. 

💡 In SOLE Colombia we have taken it to communities where it is installed in a community space where everyone can access it and decide how much, when and what it can be used for. In this way, the community regulates the data recharge according to its use and the data is used collectively to the maximum. For example, it is prioritized so that children can do homework or the community can watch a movie together, rather than using the data to access their individual social networks. But this is another challenge. The point is, you already have Internet access!

I invite you to think about how far you can go and to share it with other bangers!
